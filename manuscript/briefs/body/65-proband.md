# Nº 65 — Proband · BODY

3rd limited, Cass, past tense. Form: one harvest year, sections by the orchard calendar — bloom, thinning, harvest, dormant-season grafting; the succession paperwork (water shares, the deed, who signs) is the spine and the Huntington's inheritance the shadow ledger, never tallied aloud. ~4,500 words. Imagery: orcharding as practiced — whip-and-tongue grafting, rootstock and scion wood, budding tape, ladder work; the Wenatchee River terraces. Canon echo: a hand-pollination crew works the bloom, pollen by the gram — one line. Ending directive: Cass teaches Birdie the graft cut; her hands make it; the tape wound over the union — end on the wound tape. Forbidden: the gene test staged as a reveal (all results pre-story); the first symptom — the dropped ladder — narrated as a "moment"; the mother's dancing sentimentalized. Levers: (a) Amos's kids get tested — one letter arrives, unopened at the end; (b) Birdie's retrospective first person, the first Voss born free narrating the year Cass's hands still obeyed; (c) the brothers never reconcile — Amos signs nothing, the deed splits the orchard, the season ends, water shares divided.
