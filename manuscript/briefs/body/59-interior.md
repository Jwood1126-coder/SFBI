# Nº 59 — Interior · BODY

3rd limited, Ingrid, past tense. Form: week-numbered protocol sections; her furnished mind speaks in inventory, the store's aisles arriving one by one as the imagery takes hold. ~4,000 words. Imagery: hardware retail — SKUs, bins, fasteners by the pound, shelf facings, the oak counter and till, part numbers, stock counts; Duluth. Canon echo: the bright sky, glancing — among the things she can suddenly picture is a dark she's never seen, Rand Basin, waitlist four years, one more unlived thing. Ending directive: lying beside sleeping Petter, Ingrid pictures the store empty, then stocks it, shelf by shelf. Forbidden: the unlived lives (the daughter, the man from 2019) dramatized as flashback scenes rather than conjured images; aphantasia delivered as clinical wonder; Ingrid resolving to leave or recommit. Levers: (a) Petter asks her to describe him and the story ends inside the pause; (b) Petter's POV, the steady husband cataloguing a wife newly furnished, a man imagined for the first time; (c) darker — Ingrid asks to discontinue the protocol, the last week-header the appointment to reverse it.
