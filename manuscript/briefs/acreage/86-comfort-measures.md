# Nº 86 — Comfort Measures · ACREAGE

3rd limited, Rennie, present tense — hospice case-management rhythm applied to a county. Form: three program-phase sections (Assessment, Transition, Legacy), the clinical structure of a good death imposed on a place, so the reader watches the place palliated with the competence learned at Raleigh bedsides. ~4,500 words. Imagery: palliative practice transposed — intake, advance directives, comfort measures, the family meeting, symptom control — against coastal Carolina: the loss-running ferry, marsh, the cemetery moved uphill ahead of the water. Canon echo: Grid Rationalization — the power-triage schedule running beneath her softer one. Ending directive: the last graduation's recessional, the named song playing, the gym doors open to the sound. Forbidden: the town eulogized or nostalgized in narration, grief as scenery; the program given a cruel face, or Rennie a redemption speech about her doubt; the referendum staged as a town-hall showdown. Levers: (a) Loyd's POV, the fire chief watching his sister ease their home under; (b) one service the county fights to keep proves the one her playbook cuts first; (c) follow the remnant who stay past the last ferry.
