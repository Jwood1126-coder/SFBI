# Nº 85 — Standing · ACREAGE

3rd limited, Maren, past tense. Form braids two registers: her filings — guardian ad litem report, minimum-flow motion, setback petition, in their dry language — against field visits, where a staff gauge gives a number, the truck a road. Each reading must be drafted into a legal "want"; the gap between gauge and pleading is the story. ~4,500 words. Imagery: hydrology as practiced — cubic feet per second, stage-discharge rating curves, priority dates, Ogallala drawdown, cottonwood transects, levee setbacks on the Solomon River, Mitchell County, Kansas. Canon echo: Grid Rationalization — a neighboring county on its de-energization schedule, the triage she refuses for the river. Ending directive: the wet-spring water standing at the stage her petition named, her sister's fence line under it, the staff gauge unread. Forbidden: the river given voice, interior, or wants in narration; a courtroom showdown or a ruling delivered as speech; a sisters' reconciliation scene. Levers: (a) build it entirely from documents, assembling Maren from the margins; (b) the sister files to become co-guardian — love as legal ambush; (c) compress to one field season, one gauged reach.
