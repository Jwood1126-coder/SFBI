# Nº 79 — The Last Dark · ACREAGE

3rd limited, Maeve Corrigan, present tense. Form: the reservation book as spine — one moonless week per section; four arrivals, each on a booking penciled three years out for a sky she's calculated won't last two. ~4,500 words. Imagery: dark-sky stewardship — SQM readings in mag/arcsec², the Bortle scale, red-light discipline, satellite-train pass times; the Rand Basin playa. Canon echo: the bright sky — its load-bearing story; the four-year list and coming constellation. Ending directive: Maeve penciling the next name three years out — the pencil named, a golf pencil worn to a stub, graphite on the page's tooth. Forbidden: the Milky Way as sublime prose (the awe lives in guests' bodies, reported plainly); Maeve speechifying about the loss (the count stays in the book); the launch firm editorialized. Levers: (a) the launch scrubs — a reprieve she must decide whether to enter; (b) recast as the ledger itself — entries, cancellations, waitlist; (c) an astronomer among the four runs her math and asks whether booking three years out is kindness or con — end on her answer.
