# Nº 21 — The Verge · FAR

3rd limited, Kettle, past tense; a border-town western in the machine desert. Form: expedition-ledger chapter heads — fuel, water, nanomass assay and price — every choice priced. ~130,000 words. Imagery: salvage prospecting as trade — ore grades, spent-machine mass, assays, ration and water budgets, swarm detectors, survey markers. Canon echo: none; FAR's post-collapse world runs on its own weather. Ending directive: Kettle reaches the finished mountain-machine, reads its purpose off its plumbing and power routing — never a voice — and walks out; end on her ledger line, assay entered, value zero: the one thing on the Verge she won't sell. Forbidden: the swarm's purpose delivered as speech, recording, or text (legible only as infrastructure); the swarm given awareness of Kettle (it never notices her); the dead zone as generic wasteland — every scene runs on prices, loads, assays. Levers: (a) second POV, a border surveyor whose job is holding the line; (b) darker — she sabotages it, and the closing assay shows what it defended against already at the border; (c) compress to a single salvage season, warm machine to mountain in one run.

**The two questions.** *Outward* (how do these people survive this universe?): Can Kettle cross the Verge on the fuel, water, and nanomass her ledger allows, and read the mountain-machine's purpose before it costs her the crew? *Inward* (what must they become to do it?): What is the one thing a person who prices everything will not sell — and what does she become by walking out of the richest claim on the Verge with an assay of zero?
