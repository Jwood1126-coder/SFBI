# Nº 01 — Ballast · FAR

3rd limited, past tense, dual POV — Sana Reyes, commander, and Tomas Ilić, the machinist who stays. Alternating chapters headed by mission-elapsed clock and the deflection Δv still owed; the book reads as a rostering document that only in the last act admits a body is one of its line items. ~110,000 words, thirty chapters. Imagery: rubble-pile mining as practiced — mass-driver throughput, tether spools, shaped charges, volatiles vented for reaction mass, consumables and EVA rosters, delta-v arithmetic. Canon echo: none — a labor story; the sky's meaning would inflate a scheduling problem. Ending directive: Tomas's gloved hand on the manual tether release, the burn confirmed on the gauge; the departing ship's manifest updated — crew count, one fewer, entered. Forbidden: the noble volunteering speech; a crewmate turned mutineer over the choice; the rock rendered as an enemy. Levers: (a) single POV, Tomas throughout, Sana only from outside; (b) the wrong person is rostered to stay and the burn under-performs — a second, worse arithmetic; (c) compress to the eight months on the rock, Earth never shown.
