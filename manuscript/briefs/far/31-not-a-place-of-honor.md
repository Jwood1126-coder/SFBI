# Nº 31 — Not a Place of Honor · FAR

3rd limited, Osh, past tense; a descent whose parts are the marker rings — each part titled with that ring's warning, outer curse to inner engineering. ~120,000 words. Imagery: marker-reading as trade — glyph and pictogram interpretation, cognates, mistranslation risk, oral lore-craft; the material read without instruments — black glassy stone, thorn-field berms, radiation known only as heat and sickness. Canon echo: none; the deep-future steppe is sealed from THE GRAIN's world. Ending directive: at the warm vault threshold Osh does not break the seal; he raises the toppled marker, cuts the one sign his tribe cannot misread, and begins the warning-song for those who read danger only in story and stone — end on the song's first line, spoken, occupant unshown. Forbidden: the occupant shown or the seal broken on-page (the book ends at the threshold); the designers' intent given as a clean recording, not reconstructed from ambiguous signs; the tribes written as superstitious primitives — the curse-lore is accurate transmission. Levers: (a) interleave the ancient warning-panel's deliberations as part epigraphs, their debate over warning the ignorant against Osh's misreadings; (b) darker — rivals breach the seal off-page and the song is finished too late; (c) expand generationally, tracking the song's mutation through retellings.
