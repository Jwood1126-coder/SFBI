# Nº 11 — Bathypelagic · FAR

3rd limited, Lena Okafor, past tense. Form: dive logs interleaved so a sampling run and the mats' rerouting toward it fall chapters apart; the reader assembles the harm before the team names it. ~105,000 words. Imagery: astrobiology and electrophysiology as practiced — cryobot and hydrobot ops under the ice, filamentous biofilms and microbial nanowires, electrode arrays, planetary-protection contamination protocol, signal work separating metabolism from process. Canon echo: the Adeyemi Signature — the decades-old first proof of somebody, invoked once as the fact they thought they'd metabolized, now a nearer, worse one under the ice. Ending directive: Lena keys the array dark, logs the final impedance reading, the borehole above going quiet — the number, or its absence, no interpretation. Forbidden: the waking-god — any legible acknowledgment or message; the computation's thought decoded into human meaning; a scientist who communes with it. Levers: (a) rebuild from telemetry, the mats' activity the through-thread, human chapters interrupting; (b) the rerouting proves self-repair, not threat — the team learns they are the wound; (c) expand an Earth-side protection board as a document strand.

**The two questions.** *Outward* (how do these people survive this universe?): Can the team sample and study the biofilm without their own instruments becoming the thing that kills it? *Inward* (what must they become to do it?): What do scientists owe a mind they can perturb but never recognize — and how do you bear being the wound instead of the discoverer?
