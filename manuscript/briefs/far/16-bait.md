# Nº 16 — Bait · FAR

3rd limited, Ren Okada, past tense; three parts — Approach, Orbit, Landing — the signal maturing from prime-numbered blooms to specific welcome. ~120,000 words. Imagery: exobiology as practiced — transit spectroscopy, biosignature gases, the chlorophyll red-edge, albedo, trophic accounting, the diagnostic absence of junk and waste; honest closed-loop life support. Canon echo: none; FAR sits outside THE GRAIN's near-present canon. Ending directive: Ren feeds Teegarden c humanity's full record of waste and war; end on the continental bloom stopping mid-prime, the chlorophyll signal decaying on Ren's spectrometer — no line, no reflection. Forbidden: paradise as vague beauty or dream-vision (each lure a specific thing a named crew member wants, shown through choices); the angler embodied or scaled (inferred only, in the ecology's too-perfect cooperation); seduction by possession or mind-control. Levers: (a) rotate POV through three crew, each engineered temptation felt from inside its target; (b) tragic — Ren's own lure, to be finally understood, is the one the book cannot refute, and the ship leaves without them; (c) compress to orbit-only, no landing, the lure fought entirely through instruments.

**The two questions.** *Outward* (how do these people survive this universe?): Can the crew read the diagnostic absences — no waste, no war, no junk — and recognize the welcome as a lure before they land? *Inward* (what must they become to do it?): When the bait is the exact thing each person most wants, what must you refuse in yourself to leave — and is the wish to be finally understood a thing a person can refuse?
