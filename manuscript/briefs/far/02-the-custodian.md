# Nº 02 — The Custodian · FAR

3rd limited, Ray Osei, past tense — precise, never folksy; the prose carries his exactness. Form: parts named for the systems he keeps alive alone — WATER, POWER, AIR, COMMS, THERMAL — each learned from a dead specialist's notes, the forty returning as documented habits, torque values, marginalia. ~100,000 words. Imagery: lunar life-support maintenance as practiced — urine distillation, CO2 scrubbers, Sabatier reactors, regolith-fouled arrays, a slagged comms dish, manual overrides, dosimetry from the particle event. Canon echo: none — storm and distance seal him off; the book is one man, forty absences. Ending directive: the rescue's mass budget forces a choice: logbooks or remains; end on the item Ray straps into the return couch and the sign-off he keys, unchanged, over an empty base. Forbidden: Ray made cute — homespun aphorisms, aw-shucks; a janitor-outsmarts-the-PhDs sneer; the dead literally speaking. Levers: (a) interleave the specialists' manuals and logs as found-text headers; (b) one specialist survived, injured in a rover — now he keeps a person alive, not just systems; (c) compress to the Water crisis alone as the spine.
