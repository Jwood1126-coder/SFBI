# Nº 26 — The Unlearning · FAR

3rd limited, Sela, past tense; a librarian's thriller whose three parts are three clearance strata — novice texts, sealed chronicles, the founders' layer. ~125,000 words. Imagery: archival practice — cataloging, accession, foliation, palimpsest, mnemonic recitation drills, conservation and the founders' salted errors. Canon echo: none; FAR's long-dark world is sealed from THE GRAIN's canon. Ending directive: Sela enters a deliberate error into the master concordance — the founders' own salt — in her own hand; end on the entry and, in the margin, the sky-survey residual as a single rising number. Forbidden: the attention embodied, given a gaze, or a POV — it stays a statistic in survey data to the last page; the Order's masters written as villains (the restorationists disbelieve in good faith); an action climax — the weapon is a catalog, the violence bibliographic. Levers: (a) interleave recovered founders'-layer documents, the deliberate-collapse plan in their own voice; (b) darker — the replanting proceeds, Sela's re-salting saves a fraction, and the survey number ends higher than it began; (c) expand two generations, a later novice finding Sela's salted errors.

**The two questions.** *Outward* (how do these people survive this universe?): Can Sela pass three clearance strata to the founders' layer before the restorationists finish unlearning the archive? *Inward* (what must they become to do it?): What is a keeper of knowledge willing to become — to forge, to salt the record in her own hand — to save a fraction, when the thing above cannot be fought, only starved?
