# Nº 10 — Providence · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — what a healer is *for* when there is nothing to cure and no one to save: a doctor whose covenant is to defend the body, made to decide whether the tenant an indifferent, older universe installed in his own is a violation to fight or a charge to keep.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 27 *Fruiting Bodies*. Both grow a nonhuman organism inside human bodies toward a dispersal. Enforce the stewardship split: in *Fruiting Bodies* the body is spent substrate — reproduction by rupture, consumption, sporulation. In *Providence* the organism installs as a single working organ that integrates into and improves its host and is carried to its destination intact; the dread is teleological, not somatic — being useful cargo to something older, never being eaten. Any rupture, sprouting, or consumed host belongs to the other book.

**The two questions**
- Outward — How do six crew survive a crossing they cannot abort, on water they cannot stop drinking, with a lab meant for kidney stones, while the same mass roots into every abdomen aboard, the surgeon's included?
- Inward — What must the body's sworn defender become to read a passenger he can neither excise nor cure: someone who can price his own gut as a specimen, and decide whether arrival is a birth to permit or an occupation to refuse?

**Dyad**
- Equation — the transfer is committed: TMI burn spent, no abort-to-Earth, arrival fixed by orbital mechanics (~8-month Hohmann crossing); the lodger's maturation is phase-locked to arrival. His budget is a renal bench, one nanopore run's reagents, and light-lag to Earth climbing past twenty minutes. Two clocks that cannot be slowed, a lab that cannot be resupplied.
- Mirror — Okonkwo and Selin Petrossian, the water-reclamation engineer who keeps the system that carried the organism: the crewmate he is closest to, and the first to stop calling it an infection and call it a passenger. Her welcome against his oath is the whole crew's split worn as one bond.

**Shape** — 3rd limited, single POV (Yusuf Okonkwo), past tense. Chapters headed by the medical log — mission-elapsed time (T+ days), the reconciled count of affected crew (rising to all-hands, the line where the horror is named), and the developmental stage Okonkwo assigns the mass as he charts it, his own Carnegie-style staging. Parts divided by the crossing's phases — post-TMI cruise, mid-course, Mars approach — which are also the organ's. ~110,000 words, ~32 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Flesh — the mass vascularizes into the hepatic portal circulation, so every biopsy risks a bleed he cannot image or control and excision would exsanguinate the host; the surgical cure is off the table, and he learns it by cutting into a crewmate, then himself.
- Money/mass — the whole water inventory traces to the bunker comet; the mass budget bought no redundant Earth reserve. To stop drinking is to die of thirst, and every rationed liter reinstalls the passenger. Quarantine is impossible because the contaminant *is* the utility.
- Time/relationship — each consult with Earth's medical authority is a day-long round trip at growing light-lag, so the irreversible calls are made alone, without the cover of shared authority; the diagnosis outruns the answers, and it costs him the crew's trust.

**Science memo pointers** — Space medicine: flight-surgeon scope on long-duration missions; the real Mars-transit kit; point-of-care/tele-guided ultrasound (ISS ADUM) as the *only* imaging — no CT/MRI, operator-dependent, no soft-tissue contrast; microgravity surgery and hemostasis; confounders masking the anomaly — cephalad fluid shift, SANS, bone/muscle loss, latent-virus reactivation. Astrobiology: panspermia; radioresistance and anhydrobiosis (*Deinococcus radiodurans*, tardigrades; photolyase-driven photoreactivation defeating UV-C 254 nm at real fluence, mJ/cm²); vitrification in ice; alternative biochemistry (homochirality/D-amino acids, non-canonical bases); shadow biosphere. Water: spacecraft recovery (ISS WPA — distillation, catalytic oxidation, iodine biocide) failing against a biofilm-shielded, repair-competent organism; cometary volatiles (Rosetta/67P — D/H ratio, glycine, organics); ISRU ice mining. Lab-on-a-ship: orbital nanopore sequencing (MinION / Biomolecule Sequencer) as real hardware; field fixation and staining without a path lab; the renal bench as his toolkit. Differential: hepatic portal anatomy, liver regeneration, teratoma as the organized-but-chaotic red herring to exclude. Orbital mechanics: Hohmann Earth–Mars transfer, TMI with no free return, MOI, synodic period, ~22-min one-way light-time. Render right: photoreactivation, biofilm shielding, nanopore contamination, the impossibility of quarantine in a single-source closed loop.

**Opening image candidate** — Okonkwo ran the probe down the fourth crewman's flank, found the liver where the liver belonged and beside it the second shape, and held the wand still to count its chambers a third time against the count he had written for the two before.

**Ending directive** — Close at Mars-orbit insertion on a physical act of Okonkwo's hand, or a reading he takes and records — the one thing a physician still does when there is nothing to treat. Whether he has consented, refused, or only witnessed the disembarkation must live in the gesture; no realization, no line that names it.

**Forbidden**
- The *Alien* beat — rupture, sporulation, chest-bursting, the host consumed; that is *Fruiting Bodies*. Here the crew survive and are improved.
- The organism as antagonist — no attack, no message, no reveal of makers or purpose. It stays biology, indifferent, inferred only from instruments; explaining it kills the dread.
- The outbreak thriller — patient zero, race for a cure, quarantine drama. Act I refutes it: everyone is affected, it is the water, there is no epidemiology plot to run.
- The apotheosis ending — clean transcendence, worship, "we were the disease." The improvement must cost, and the split stays clinical.
- The mad-doctor spiral — Okonkwo's competence holds; the scariest page is a nominal exam with one line wrong, not delirium.

**Levers** — (a) dual POV, adding Petrossian or the commander to externalize the split; or an epistolary spine of his logs sent Earthward against the lag, every answer a day stale. (b) darker: the improvement is real and one-way, and those who accept arrive unable to return to Earth's biosphere, made Mars-adapted cargo; warmer: the passenger is a true symbiont and the book becomes reconciliation, learning to steward rather than defend. (c) scope: contract to his own abdomen, the self as the only locked room; or widen to Mars arrival and the base's quarantine politics.
