# Nº 10 — Providence · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — what a healer is *for* when there is nothing to cure and no one to save: a doctor sworn to defend the body, forced to decide whether the tenant an older, indifferent universe installed in his own is a violation to fight or a charge to keep.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 27 *Fruiting Bodies*. Both grow a nonhuman organism inside human bodies toward a dispersal. Enforce the stewardship split: in *Fruiting Bodies* the host is spent substrate — reproduction by rupture and sporulation; in *Providence* the organism installs as one working organ, integrates, improves its host, and rides to its destination intact. The dread is teleological, not somatic — being useful cargo to something older, never being eaten. Any rupture or consumed host belongs to the other book.

**The two questions**
- Outward — How do six crew survive a crossing they cannot abort, on water they cannot stop drinking, with a lab meant for kidney stones, while the same mass roots into every abdomen aboard — the surgeon's included?
- Inward — What must the body's sworn defender become to read a passenger he can neither excise nor cure: someone who can price his own gut as a specimen, and decide whether arrival is a birth to permit or an occupation to refuse?

**Dyad**
- Equation — the transfer is committed: TMI spent, no abort-to-Earth, arrival fixed by a ~8-month Hohmann crossing, and the organism's maturation phase-locked to it. His whole budget: a renal bench, one nanopore run's reagents, a light-lag past twenty minutes. Two clocks he cannot slow, a lab he cannot resupply.
- Mirror — Okonkwo and Selin Petrossian, the water-reclamation engineer whose system carried the organism and the first to stop calling it infection and call it passenger. Her welcome against his oath is the crew's whole split worn as one bond.

**Shape** — 3rd limited, single POV (Yusuf Okonkwo), past tense. Chapters headed by the medical log: mission-elapsed time, the reconciled count of affected crew (climbing to all-hands — the line where the horror is named), and the developmental stage he assigns the mass, his own Carnegie-style staging. Parts follow the crossing's phases — post-TMI cruise, mid-course, Mars approach — which are also the organ's. ~110,000 words, ~32 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Flesh — the mass vascularizes into the hepatic portal circulation: every biopsy risks a bleed he cannot image or stop, excision would exsanguinate the host. The surgical cure is gone — and he learns it by cutting a crewmate, then himself.
- Money/mass — the whole water inventory traces to the bunker comet; the mass budget bought no redundant Earth reserve. To stop drinking is to die of thirst, and every rationed liter reinstalls the passenger. Quarantine is impossible because the contaminant *is* the utility.
- Time/relationship — each Earth consult is a day-long round trip at growing lag, so the irreversible calls are made alone, without the cover of shared authority; the diagnosis outruns the answers, and it costs him the crew's trust.

**Science memo pointers** — Space medicine: flight-surgeon scope on long-duration missions; tele-guided point-of-care ultrasound (ISS ADUM) as the *only* imaging — no CT/MRI, operator-dependent, no soft-tissue contrast; microgravity surgery and hemostasis; confounders masking the anomaly (cephalad fluid shift, SANS, bone/muscle loss, latent-virus reactivation). Astrobiology: panspermia; radioresistance and anhydrobiosis (*Deinococcus radiodurans*, tardigrades; photolyase photoreactivation defeating UV-C 254 nm at real fluence, mJ/cm²); vitrification in ice; homochirality/non-canonical bases; shadow biosphere. Water: spacecraft recovery (ISS WPA — distillation, catalytic oxidation, iodine biocide) failing against a biofilm-shielded, repair-competent organism; cometary volatiles (Rosetta/67P — D/H ratio, glycine). Lab-on-a-ship: orbital nanopore sequencing (MinION / Biomolecule Sequencer); field fixation and staining without a path lab. Differential: hepatic portal anatomy, liver regeneration, teratoma as the organized-but-chaotic red herring to exclude. Orbital mechanics: Hohmann transfer, TMI with no free return, MOI, ~22-min one-way light-time.

**Opening image candidate** — Okonkwo ran the probe down the fourth crewman's flank, found the liver where the liver belonged and beside it the second shape, and held the wand still to count its chambers a third time against the count he had written for the two before.

**Ending directive** — Close at Mars-orbit insertion on a physical act of his hand, or a reading he takes and records — the one thing a physician still does when there is nothing to treat. Whether he has consented, refused, or only witnessed the disembarkation must live in the gesture; no realization, no line that names it.

**Forbidden**
- The *Alien* beat — rupture, sporulation, a host consumed; that is *Fruiting Bodies*. Here the crew survive and are improved.
- The organism as antagonist — no attack, no message, no reveal of makers or purpose; it stays indifferent biology, inferred only from instruments.
- The outbreak thriller — patient zero, race for a cure, quarantine drama. Act I refutes it: everyone is affected, it is the water.
- The apotheosis ending — clean transcendence, worship, "we were the disease." The improvement must cost; the split stays clinical, the competence intact.

**Levers** — (a) dual POV — add Petrossian or the commander to externalize the split; or an epistolary spine of logs sent Earthward, every answer a day stale. (b) darker: the improvement is one-way, and those who accept arrive unable to return to Earth's biosphere, Mars-adapted cargo; warmer: the passenger is a true symbiont and the book becomes reconciliation, stewardship over defense. (c) scope: contract to his own abdomen, the self as the only locked room; or widen to Mars arrival and the base's quarantine politics.
