# Nº 05 — Perennial · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — a maker who can no longer author, only negotiate: the passage from designing a living thing to consenting to be used by it, and whether that surrender is defeat or the only love left.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 03 *The Greenhouse Variable*. The near-twin: both are lone woman-biologists in the same Vol I isolated-life-support setup, watching engineered life drift toward an impossible-rate fixation metabolism (Nº 10 *Providence* is the distant medical cousin — one exogenous organ in one abdomen — not the confusable one). Enforce the one split that matters, reciprocal to what Brief 03 fences: *Perennial*'s fixation is authorless emergence — founder drift and GCR mutation, no malice at all, no author anywhere; *Greenhouse*'s is deliberate design with a maker inbound. The instant the "impossible fixation" reads as engineered — a payload, a watermark, someone who meant it — this becomes Greenhouse. Keep it accident all the way down.

**The two questions** — Outward: how do 801 people cross ninety years inside a sealed loop that has reclassified its sleepers as its richest untapped nutrient stock? Inward: can a designer stop correcting her creation and become a compartment inside it — load-bearing, metabolized-into, no longer standing outside the system she drew?

**Dyad** — Equation: 180 waking days against two coupled curves — the hibernation bay's chemistry drifting toward a lethal threshold as the loop's new carbon-fixer draws down a shared nutrient; every move in one shifts the other. Mirror: Rahel Fenn, who co-designed the biosphere with her and now sleeps in the bay that fails first — the person she built it with is the body the loop is balancing against.

**Shape** — 3rd limited, single POV (Dana Voss), past tense. Chapters headed by the day's assay ledger: shift-day plus one measured value from loop or bay, the numbers drifting across the book so the clock reads in real units. ~110,000 words, ~32 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Mass/food: an intervention's reagents and competitor biomass come only from the arrival ration reserve — she spends the colony's future food, or her own six-month margin, on a correction that may not hold.
- Relationship: prioritizing failing pods is triage; and making the sleepers load-bearing means binding Rahel's dormant body into the loop as a node — consent she cannot wake her to ask.
- Time/metabolism: she cannot share the work without waking a second ecologist and doubling the human load on a loop already pricing humans as feedstock.

**Science memo pointers** — Closed ecological life support (BIOS-3, MELiSSA; Biosphere 2's oxygen-into-concrete decline as the model failure); microbial evolution in tiny founders (founder effect, Muller's ratchet); GCR radiation biology and dosimetry as the mutation driver; alternative carbon fixation (RuBisCO vs. Wood–Ljungdahl, reductive TCA) — the "impossible" fixation must map to a real pathway; induced-torpor biochemistry and its failure modes (acidosis, electrolyte and pH drift); ecological stoichiometry (Redfield ratios, N/P limitation, mass balance); in-situ instruments — nanopore metagenomics, flow cytometer, GC/MS, spectrophotometer, dissolved-gas probes. Render right: trophic cascade, keystone-removal collapse, anoxia, ammonia spike. A 90-year crossing carries its own delta-v and light-lag; keep both consistent offstage.

**Opening image candidate** — First shift, first draw: she pulled six milliliters off the reclamation loop, ran it through the spectrophotometer she had calibrated before launch, and read a fixation rate no organism on her manifest could account for.

**Ending directive** — Close on a logged measurement, not a stated decision: her own body entered into the census as a compartment of the loop she has re-authored. Whether she keys the couch or turns from it, end on the ledger line and the reading — not her face, not a verdict.

**Forbidden**
- The biosphere given a mind, a will, or a voice; its agency is statistical and metabolic, never intent.
- The garden as wonder-jungle; awe arrives through instruments read correctly, never through adjectives.
- The nutrient-reserve threat as body horror — dissolving pods, a creature; keep it an ammonia number, a drifting assay.
- A helpful ship-mind or companion AI; she works the instruments alone.
- The "playing God" speech, or a scene where she finally solves the food web; the loop stays partly unreadable to the end.

**Levers** — (a) wake the next-shift ecologist early for a second POV, two designers split on fighting versus steering the evolution. (b) darker: the drift is already past threshold at thaw and pods are lost — the book becomes triage, not prevention. (c) scope: predecessors' shift-logs surface as found text — she is not the first to wake into this, and one before her chose differently.
