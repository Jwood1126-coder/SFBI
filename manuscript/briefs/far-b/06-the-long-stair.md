# Nº 06 — The Long Stair · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — inheritance as vertigo: whether the grandchildren of a saving catastrophe are allowed to want back the sky their elders cut down, and whether reaching for it honors the dead or cancels their mercy.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 21 *The Verge*. Both are salvage-culture expeditions — unmanufacturable material, rival crews, a ledger economy — toward a terminal mega-construction and a final choice. Enforce the axis: Verge is horizontal, a western of prices and raiders; The Long Stair is vertical, its antagonist the atmospheric column and dead machinery, never a market. If the climb becomes a heist, it is Verge's book.

**The two questions** — Outward: how does a crew that has only ever worked at the base keep human bodies alive up four hundred kilometers of dead, unpressurized carbon rail — troposphere to vacuum — on salvaged climbers and a current someone above still draws? Inward: what must a scavenger become to climb the dead world's greatest work instead of stripping it, and to answer, at the top, whether her grandmother's cut was a mercy to keep or a door to reopen?

**Dyad** — Equation: a one-way climb budget — ~400 km at a rate set by salvaged motors and rail current, against a consumables clock (O₂, water, battery) and a survivability floor that drops with altitude (unpressurized death above the Armstrong limit, vacuum by the Kármán line), while the station's rising current draw sets an arrival clock that will not wait. Mirror: Mireya, Ita's grandmother, crew on the severing sixty years ago, dying at the base on a radio that thins as Ita rises, begging her to cut it again. The woman she climbs away from is the one whose single irreversible act she may undo.

**Shape** — Single POV, close third on Ita, past tense. Three parts by atmospheric layer (Troposphere, Stratosphere, Vacuum), a fourth at the station. Chapters headed by altitude (km) and a three-line status panel — cabin partial pressure, consumables remaining, rail-current draw — where the one wrong line is the dread. ~120,000 words, ~33 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Flesh: above the Armstrong limit the unpressurized body is finished; the crew pays in hypoxia, frostbite, decompression, and a partner lost high on the rail stays there — a body is mass no one carries down.
- Mass/money: the ascent is bankrolled by stripping the ribbon they climb, spending irreplaceable super-carbon on a one-way rig; fail, and rivals take the rig and the base has nothing.
- Time: Ita spends the last weeks of Mireya's life out of radio reach, climbing away from the person whose meaning her hands at the top may erase.

**Science memo pointers** — Space-elevator engineering (Artsutanov/Pearson; NASA NIAC Edwards–Westling; GEO 35,786 km; the CNT/graphene specific-strength gap — the novum is bulk near-theoretical carbon ribbon). The problem the researcher MUST solve first: a tether cut at 30,000 km leaves no taut, standing 400 km base, so build a defensible tension source (a surviving upper remnant on an apex mass; a re-anchored node) or the ascent is unmotivated. Severance dynamics: the fall and whip, the upper segment migrating outward, TSS-1R (STS-75), Coriolis on a climber. Atmospheric column: barometric formula, Armstrong limit (~19 km), Kármán line (100 km), O₂ partial pressure, time of useful consciousness, HAPE/HACE and the death zone, the crossover from mountaineering physiology to pressure-suit engineering. Portable ECLSS mass budget (CO₂ scrubbing, O₂, water). Sixty-year vacuum failures: cold-welding, outgassing, embrittlement, fatigue, micrometeoroid and atomic-oxygen erosion. Electrified-rail current and beamed-power climbers; decades of astrometry/radar on a decelerating inbound, off-page.

**Opening image candidate** — She dragged the meter's probes down the dead climber rail the way her mother had taught her, expecting the pinned needle, and watched it climb.

**Ending directive** — End on Ita's hands and one irreversible motion at the threshold — the cut re-made against the live rail, or the console answered — the inbound's range still closing on the panel, the meaning plain in the doing, never argued, whatever waits never shown.

**Forbidden**
- The mountaineering-triumph arc: summit-as-catharsis, a planted flag, a partner's death as inspiration. To the top it is a rostering-and-physics problem, and death is mass.
- The salvage-western (Verge fence): rival crews as raiders, the climb as heist. They are labor competitors, not villains.
- The decode-the-warning puzzle box (Honor fence): the logs as a designed message. They are operational wreckage; the antagonist is physics, not interpretation.
- The inbound thing shown or explained, or the station's sensors written as a HAL with an agenda. The dread is the closing range and the drawn current.
- Mireya's cut moralized as wisdom or cowardice, the "reached too far" sermon. It stays undecidable to the last page.

**Levers** — (a) Dual strand: braid Mireya's severance crew sixty years back against Ita's ascent, the old descending as the young rise. (b) Darker/warmer: darker, the top offers no choice and no way down, current and inbound indifferent to her; warmer, she brings the crew down alive with what they learned and leaves the door for later. (c) Scope: compress to the single climb, near-real-time; or widen to a season on the Line and rival crews' own ascents.
