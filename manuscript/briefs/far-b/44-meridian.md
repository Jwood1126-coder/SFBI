# Nº 44 — Parallax · FAR (Tier B)
*(dossier title "Parallax" kept; ship renamed Meridian→Lodestar — see Registry)*

1. **Secretly about** — a people whose entire identity is *being in motion toward something* discovering they stopped generations ago, and having to decide whether to move again as a free choice rather than an inherited debt.

2. **Sibling rule** — Nº 47 *The Ark of Zero*. Both end on an ancient intelligence that shepherds species' arks across deep time. Enforce: 47 is an open-space exodus fleeing a real, moving death; 44 never leaves the jar — stationary, claustrophobic, its keeper an intimate examiner, never a fleet-scale herder. Any scene showing the wider galaxy or a second ark under way belongs to 47.

3. **The two questions** — *Outward:* how does a five-generation crew prove a counterfeit sky from inside it, and reach the door before their own civil order eats the ship? *Inward:* what must a civilization built on the sacred trajectory become when the trajectory is exposed as finished — can it *elect* the voyage it once merely inherited?

4. **Dyad** — *Equation:* the proof integrates against a fixed sensor noise floor at a rate no one can hurry — the parallax residual crosses detectability on a schedule of months — while the ship's political half-life is shorter; certainty and collapse race, and collapse is ahead. *Mirror:* Suli and her master navigator, teacher and apprentice on opposite banks of the correction-versus-heresy schism, bound by love of the one craft tearing the ship apart.

5. **Shape** — Single close third on Suli Anand; past tense. Structural device: each chapter heads with a dated navigation-log line pairing the catalog's *predicted* value against the *observed*, the O−C residual creeping off zero — dread as a nominal checklist with one line wrong. Three parts (the acts). ~120,000 words, ~34 chapters.

6. **The novum's price**
   - *Flesh.* An honest baseline needs an instrument the counterfeit doesn't cover, deployed hull-side beyond the standard suite. The EVA to place it costs a crewmate or a hand; the proof is bought in vacuum.
   - *The social contract.* Rationing, the labor draft, and the birth lottery were all justified by the crossing. Exposing the voyage as over voids the justification; the schism kills, and the master — whose authority rested on the Track — is among the dead or the broken.
   - *The unborn's account.* Passing the interview means willingly resuming ~160 more years of dark and ration, surrendering the comfort of *we have arrived* — a life staked on a door that may not be survivable, paid by the living for people not yet born.

7. **Science memo pointers** — Astrometry: stellar parallax (arcsec ↔ parsec), proper motion, Gaia-class µas precision, and the observables a fake sky must reproduce and will leak on — stellar aberration (v/c), relativistic Doppler/beaming at cruise fraction of c, gravitational deflection near the target. O−C (observed-minus-computed) residual diagrams as the plot's literal instrument; Hipparcos/Gaia-style catalogs, DE-series ephemerides. The absolute reference a forger is likeliest to botch: X-ray pulsar navigation (SEXTANT/NICER XNAV), pulsar timing residuals, atomic-clock stability (Allan deviation), barycentric time. Ship physics: "no deceleration felt" holds because a spun habitat gives spin gravity, not thrust gravity (rotation radius vs. rpm for target g, Coriolis limits); delta-v budget and cruise velocity implied by a 300-year crossing. Closed-loop ECLSS (MELiSSA, BIOS-3): O₂/CO₂ balance, water recovery, crop area and energy per head, 300-year consumables margin. Multigenerational demographics: effective population size, minimum viable population, inbreeding, banked gametes. Failure modes: astrometric systematics (plate scale, detector), integration against noise, aliasing, the blind spots a forger exploits.

8. **Opening image candidate** — At the close of the middle watch Suli Anand read the reference star off the plate, subtracted the ephemeris, and entered the difference in the O−C column, where the number had stood at zero longer than anyone aboard had been alive.

9. **Ending directive** — An act at the navigator's station that rhymes with the opening measurement — a course committed, or one line written into the residual column — so the last number is a *chosen* one, not an inherited one. Do not fix whether they pass, what the keeper does, or what waits past the door.

10. **Forbidden**
    - Showing the keeper: no body, no face, no motive speech, no dialogue — the interview is inferred, the collector absent to the last page.
    - The enclosure as a third-act gotcha. Orient the reader on the fact early; the mystery is what it *means* and costs, not whodunit.
    - Generation-ship stock: a crew that forgot it's on a ship, cryopods and sleepers, a rogue AI, mutiny in steel corridors, faith-versus-reason as cardboard.
    - The choice to keep going as rousing consensus or a captain's inspirational address; the passing grade is ambivalent, costly, paid.
    - A magically perfect fake sky — it must fail in specific, catchable, physical ways or the procedural is a cheat.

11. **Levers** — (a) Dual POV: add the master navigator or a Waykeeper of the Track as a second head carrying the political-collapse axis, with liturgy/log interchapters. (b) Darker/warmer: darker — the jar holds ships of species that chose to stop, and Suli boards a warning-hulk; warmer — a neighbor ark that already passed, quiet contact across the glass. (c) Scope: contract to a bottle piece in one instrument bay across a single long integration; or widen into the first years of the re-elected voyage.
