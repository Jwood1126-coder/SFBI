# Nº 24 — Under the Veil · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — what a generation owes the desperate mercy that saved it by taking the sky, and whether the one person who can read that mercy as a failing machine can bear to become the apostate who takes it off.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 33 *Shade*. Both dim Earth under an engineered shield reached by operating an old control system whose use exposes us to something outside. Enforce the authorship split: *Shade*'s shade is alien wreckage and being-seen is active signaling to its shatterer; here the Veil is *ours* — a mercy we built, worship, and forgot — the antagonist is our own descendants' faith, and being-seen is passive, our hidden biosignature unmasked. If the Veil ever reads as externally imposed, or the watcher becomes a party the plot bargains with, it has become *Shade*.

**The two questions**
- Outward — Can a discredited atmospheric engineer cross a continent of Veil-worshipping territories, restore a mothballed launch stack the faithful guard as a shrine, and reach the orbital node before the shield's uneven decay fires a patchwork termination shock across the only climate anyone alive has known?
- Inward — What must a woman raised in the faith become to stop *tending* the mercy and unmake it — to trade the consolation and the safety of hiding for a clarity her own people will read as the end of the world?

**Dyad**
- Equation — The Veil's decay is spatially heterogeneous: optical depth τ collapsing fastest in some stratospheric cells while others hold, so termination-shock warming arrives per region on a decade curve, not globally at once — against one restored stack with a single propellant-and-window budget and an ascent that must plane-match the node's inclination. Every month spent converting the faithful is warming banked in the failing cells.
- Mirror — Arash Mun, her brother, keeper of the launch-shrine: blood on both sides of the apostasy — the man whose whole life is guarding the thing she has come to desecrate, and the one believer she must convert or betray to go up.

**Shape** — Close third, single POV (Skyla Mun), past tense. The book ascends: parts ordered by altitude — *under the pearl* (the city of the faith), *across the territories* (the pilgrimage), *up the Gantry* (the restored stack), *the Cornice* (orbit). Chapter heads pair a forbidden instrument reading — an optical-depth τ, a star's magnitude, a spectrometer line — against a line of the faith's liturgy: two epistemologies side by side, the numbers winning by inches. ~120,000 words, ~34 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Flesh/standing — reading the failure needs banned instruments (spectrometers, star charts sold as heresy); operating them costs Skyla her license, her congregation, her liberty, and the thinning cells dose her with the ozone-hole UV the Veil no longer stops — skin, eyes, paid in her own body.
- Mass/money/a life — the mothballed stack needs cryogenic propellant no lawful supplier will sell; fueling it cannibalizes the pilgrimage's stores and kills someone in the making of it (a cryo/LOX handling death), and every kilogram to the node is subtracted from consumables.
- Relationship/species — to save the surviving climate she must strip the Veil, and stripping it un-hides Earth's biosignature: she buys the world's future by spending its invisibility, and buys the node by breaking Arash's faith, or Arash.

**Science memo pointers** — Solar radiation management / stratospheric aerosol injection: sulfate (SO₂→H₂SO₄ droplets) vs. calcite/alumina at ~20 km; aerosol optical depth, single-scattering albedo, Mie scattering, and the whitened "pearl" sky (lost Rayleigh-blue, direct→diffuse shift). Load-bearing failure mode: *termination shock* — abrupt warming rebound if forcing stops; model it cell-by-cell, not global. Touchstones: Pinatubo 1991 (~0.5 °C cooling, measured ozone loss), SCoPEx, the Keith / MacMartin / Kravitz SRM literature, GeoMIP. Chronic sulfate → catalytic stratospheric ozone depletion and ground UV (the failing cells as UV hotspots); SAI's ITCZ/monsoon shifts and diffuse-light agronomy as why territories farm and fear the Veil differently. The self-maintaining swarm: high-altitude platforms — stratospheric balloons, SAIL-class tankers, solar HAPS — under an orbital command/telemetry segment (the Cornice). Ascent rocketry: restoring a mothballed cryogenic stack (LOX/LH₂ or kerolox), boil-off, launch azimuth and inclination-matching, delta-v against a single window; cryo/hypergolic handling deaths as the real hazard. The being-seen physics: transit and direct-imaging spectroscopy, biosignatures (O₂/O₃ Chappuis band, CH₄ disequilibrium, the vegetation red edge) and how a uniform high-albedo veil flattens Earth's spectrum to a dead "pale white dot" — the founders' second reason. Keep SETI/technosignature framing offstage; the reality law rides the shield, not the audience.

**Opening image candidate** — By lamp she files the stripped fitting until the banned spectrometer seats against the shutter, and reads, for the ninth night running, the optical depth the pearl is not supposed to have.

**Ending directive** — Close at the Cornice on a single act of her hand on a control that holds or releases the swarm, witnessed — and on a first, plainly named sight (a star, the true sun, the dark) spoken by someone who has never seen it. Whether unveiling is deliverance or exposure must live in the gesture and the naming; leave unresolved who, if anyone, is watching.

**Forbidden**
- The swarm as villain, the Veil as sky-god with intent: it is infrastructure doing its maintenance job — no malevolent AI, no machine that "wants" the sky shut.
- The geoengineering-hubris sermon: the founders were not fools and the Veil was not a sin — it saved the world; the tragedy is a right answer that became a cage. Any "we should never have" moralizing kills it.
- The faithful as strawman Luddites: the religion is an intelligent, working adaptation, and the shrine-keepers grasp a cost Skyla underrates — the safety of hiding.
- The reveal delivered on-screen — an alien, a message, a fleet. The watcher stays unshown; the horror is the exposed spectrum, not a monster.
- The stars-return finale as unmixed triumph: unveiling is also loss — the consolation that held society, and the camouflage that kept Earth quiet. No clean sunrise.

**Levers** — (a) Dual POV, Skyla against Arash — engineering and liturgy alternating chapter for chapter; or a mosaic of the pilgrimage territories, each a different bargain with the Veil. (b) Darker: the Veil can neither be saved nor safely removed, and the book becomes who chooses to be seen and dies for it; warmer: Skyla converts the faith rather than defeats it, and the ascent becomes a congregation's act — the shrine launching its own god down. (c) Compress to a siege/heist for the Gantry alone, near-real-time, for clock; or widen to a generational frame where one Mun diagnoses the failure and the next flies the ascent, for meaning.
