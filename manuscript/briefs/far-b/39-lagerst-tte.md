# Nº 39 — Lagerstätte · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — testimony against the outside view: that a species read entirely from its remains can still be spoken for, and the deepest act available to a lifelong reader of the dead is to stop excavating and answer the record on behalf of the living.

**Sibling rule** — Most in danger of resembling **Nº 31 Not a Place of Honor** — both turn on a deep-time mark left for a later mind, read as survival. Split on address: 31's people are the intended readers, decoding a warning built for them; 39's discover they are the *subject*, not the reader, of a report filed about them — and 39's record, unlike 31's fixed markers, is still open, still transmitting, amendable. Keep 39 on being judged and answering back, never on decipherment for its own sake.

**The two questions**
- Outward — how does a field team survive being nationalized onto the most secured hole on Earth long enough to decode a still-transmitting beacon and answer it, before the assessors it summoned in 1945 decelerate into range?
- Inward — what must a scientist trained to infer the dead without ever speaking for them become, to speak for the living — to stake her species on her own testimony?

**Dyad**
- Equation — the arrival is fixed and computable: the beacon woke at the first atmospheric fission signature, the response has braked inbound at a knowable fraction of c ever since, and light-lag plus the deceleration curve set an ETA no one can move — the only free variable is what the report says when read.
- Mirror — Molnar and the surveyor, the only two beings ever to work this trade on this planet, excavator and excavated across sixty-six million years — she is his sole mourner, and that recognition, not her clearance, earns her the standing to write back.

**Shape** — Braided limited third: past tense in the modern strand (Molnar), present tense in the deep-time strand, which is constrained reconstruction that never exceeds what the dig has earned. Modern chapters head with the beacon's transmission log and the assessor's closing range (km/s, days); deep-time chapters with the accession and stratum that license them. ~120,000 words, ~34 chapters, three parts.

**The novum's price**
- **Flesh (the specimen):** every fact is destructive — CT and synchrotron reach a limit, so reading the beacon lattice and the body's chemistry means sectioning the one specimen; each answer unmakes the only evidence, and each deep-time chapter holds only what the cutting has already destroyed.
- **Relationship (classification):** the instant the beacon transmits the dig is nationalized; Molnar trades the open discipline that made her — provenance, publication, her students' authorship — for a clearance that forbids her to speak, and signs the paper that disappears the colleague who leaks.
- **Time (the fixed clock):** the ETA cannot be bought back; the amendment window is a set count of weeks, and adding anything true to the report means gathering and transmitting fresh data about us that costs something real to obtain.

**Science memo pointers** — Taphonomy / Konservat-Lagerstätten: Hell Creek Formation (Late Maastrichtian, ~66 Ma), K–Pg boundary clay and iridium anomaly, Chicxulub, the Tanis impact-day deposit (real, contested — cite the controversy). Fossilization: permineralization, recrystallization, which geometry outlasts which chemistry; the Schweitzer soft-tissue debate as the bar the airtight paleontology must clear. Dating: U–Pb zircons and ⁴⁰Ar/³⁹Ar on bracketing bentonite ash beds, magnetostratigraphy. Instruments: micro-CT and synchrotron X-ray tomographic microscopy (ESRF/Diamond/SLS scale), SEM–EDS, Raman, stable isotopes. Trigger: the atmospheric markers of the nuclear age — ¹⁴C bomb pulse, ²³⁹Pu and ¹³⁷Cs fallout (the Anthropocene GSSP / Crawford Lake debate), Trinity, July 1945, as t-zero. Approach: sub-relativistic deceleration, light-travel time constraining the responder to ≲ ~40 ly for the plot's clock, delta-v of a course change, comms lag. Forensics: paleopathology, cause-of-death inference, comparative functional morphology of a bipedal non-human (biomechanics from joint geometry, no overreach). Institutions: fossil-ownership law on federal vs. private land (the Murray / "Dueling Dinosaurs" precedent), chain of custody, the IAA post-detection protocols — invoked, then overridden by classification. Reality law binds hardest here: paleontology and orbital arithmetic must both survive a specialist, so the one implausibility spends only on the beacon.

**Opening image candidate** — Molnar ran the trowel along the radius a third time because bone does not carry a seam, and the shale gave up the same answer it had given twice: a cuff, sealed, at the wrist of something that had worn it.

**Ending directive** — End on the act of amendment — Molnar entering the last true thing into a record built to be read and never answered — with the beacon's acceptance or refusal rendered only as a state change: a cadence, a light. Close on the entry made, not the verdict it earns or the assessors' reply; the book ends at the threshold of judgment, not inside it.

**Forbidden**
- The assessors shown — faces, motives, a spoken verdict; they stay trajectory and consequence, and the book ends at the threshold, not in the courtroom.
- Ancient-astronaut schlock: the fossil played for twist-shock or a von Däniken wink; the paleontology stays airtight, the wonder arrives through instruments read correctly.
- Conspiracy-thriller mode — black SUVs, a shadowy general, action set-pieces; the dread is procedural, a form with one line wrong, not a chase.
- The surveyor humanized — interiority, a family, a home he misses; the deep-time strand stays bounded to evidence and stays alien.
- Humanity "proving itself worthy" by a redemptive gesture, or the amendment as a feel-good speech; the moral question stays open and costly, the verdict never shown to be favorable.

**Levers**
- (a) Give the deep-time strand to the surveyor's own bounded POV — an alien field scientist's log of a world's last day — as a true second protagonist; warmer, riskier, courts the humanizing trap, pull only with an iron rule for what the fossil licenses.
- (b) Fork, pick one: the amendment fails (the record was read-only, the final update already sent) and the book becomes acting rightly with no power over the verdict; or the surveyor's report already argued *for* us, and the assessors are inbound to protect, not judge.
- (c) Scope in — never cut to the assessors; the whole novel stays in quarry and lab, the cosmic scale arriving only as a range number ticking down (the Nº 14 move).

**Registry** — Protagonist **Dr. Petra Molnar** (dossier-given): "Petra" is zero-hit among corpus protagonists (the only strings are unrelated surnames Petrash, ch12, and Petranek, ch04); "Molnar" appears only in this premise's dossier — clean. The **surveyor**, **assessors**, and **beacon** stay common nouns by design (no invented proper noun; honors the three-term slang cap). Real geography only — the Hell Creek Formation. No vessel, station, company, or drug introduced; no renames.
