# Nº 36 — Second Eden · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — whether tending a world all the way includes the will to unmake it: a builder who finds his predecessors razed their own Eden on purpose, and must decide whether finishing his is devotion or their mistake run twice.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 41 *Regolith*. Both wake an ancient dormant biology that reactivates on liquid water and an oxidizing atmosphere and treats terrestrial biochemistry as feedstock. Enforce the one split: Regolith is an outbreak/containment thriller with the ecology as antagonist and epidemiology as engine; Second Eden keeps the waking remnant almost wholly off-page and makes the antagonist the *choice to sterilize* — a stewardship tragedy about becoming your predecessors. The moment Mars's biology becomes a thing fought or quarantined scene by scene, it is Regolith.

**The two questions**
- *Outward:* How does a terraforming crew arrest — or complete — an irreversible planetary thaw once the warming's own success is waking what the crust was cooled to contain, with the first open-air settlers already on the ground?
- *Inward:* What must a man who spent his life making a home become to hold the tool that ends one — to be capable of the unmaking he arrived to prosecute as a crime?

**Dyad**
- *Equation:* the activation isotherm descends through the Martian cryosphere on the committed warming curve, reaching the deep aquifer on a calculable date; planetary thermal inertia means the stored heat cannot be pulled back out in time — the crew can add heat faster (finish) or watch it descend regardless (there is no reverse).
- *Mirror:* Chaudhary and Deniz Vardar, the two who staked their lives on green Mars, now reading the same cores to opposite verdicts — the partnership that built the world is the thing the choice costs.

**Shape** — Two POV, close third: Anand Chaudhary (terraforming director; the budget, the politics, the inward arc) and Deniz Vardar (exo-paleobiogeochemist; reader of the first Eden and finder of the remnant, the foil who reaches the opposite verdict). Past tense. Chapter heads carry the terraforming dashboard — mean surface temperature (K), atmospheric pressure (mbar), modeled activation-isotherm depth, days-to-first-open-air — one warming driving a triumph clock and a doom clock at once; in the final part the triumph clock reaches zero and is struck, leaving only days-to-activation. ~125,000 words, ~36 chapters, four parts.

**The novum's price**
- *Time/money:* the warming is already spent — comet volatiles delivered, the algal albedo feedback compounding, the crust heat-charged. A halt cannot refreeze a planet on any human timescale; it forfeits a generation and a civilization-scale budget while the isotherm descends anyway. The decision was never reversible, and learning that is the first cost.
- *Flesh:* to know whether the aquifer remnant is alive and what it is, someone must breach and sample it directly, under a planetary-protection regime that breaks on contact — exposure, biocontainment, a body that cannot come back up clean. A death, or a life quarantined for good, is the price of the reading.
- *Relationship:* the reading splits Chaudhary and Vardar to opposite verdicts, and either final act murders a world's worth of people — the settlers the project was for, or the biosphere it woke. No choice here is paid in less than everything.

**Science memo pointers** — Terraforming feasibility / CO₂ budget: Jakosky & Edwards (2018), insufficient accessible CO₂ with present inventories; residual south-polar CO₂ cap, regolith-adsorbed CO₂, CO₂ clathrate, present ~6 mbar vs. a several-hundred-mbar target, the Armstrong limit (~63 mbar), breathing O₂ at ~160 mbar-equivalent. The one implausibility is imported volatiles — cometary/outer-body water, N₂, ammonia — so nail Δv, capture, and impact-heating energy until it survives an orbital engineer. Cryosphere/geothermics: km-scale ice-cemented cryosphere, low heat flow (~19–30 mW/m²), geothermal gradient, thermal diffusivity of frozen regolith and the decades-to-centuries descent time of a warming isotherm; perchlorate-brine eutectics lowering the freezing point; the contested MARSIS south-polar basal-water detection (Orosei et al. 2018), RSL, deep briny aquifers. Reading a prior biosphere in strata: δ¹³C and δ³⁴S (incl. mass-independent) fractionation, banded redox/oxidation fronts, stromatolite morphology, lipid biomarkers (hopanes, steranes) and preservation limits, and the abiotic-mimic problem that is the real work. Chronostratigraphy: the Amazonian (~3 Gy–present) makes 300 Mya defensible; radiometric dating of returned samples (K-Ar, Rb-Sr, cosmogenic nuclides) vs. crater counts that cannot resolve it. Planetary protection is load-bearing — COSPAR forward/back-contamination categories, curation — humans and the remnant being mutual contaminants. First-sterilization mechanism: Mars is self-sterilizing now (UV, perchlorates/oxychlorines, peroxides, GCR/SPE), so terraforming switches off the native biocide; the predecessors' unmaking is pulling the greenhouse to let sterility return, or re-oxidizing the regolith globally. Ground the first-breath campaign in real ECLSS and settlement physiology — partial pressures, engineered-cyanobacteria O₂ productivity, the centuries-true timeline.

**Opening image candidate** — Chaudhary broke the wax on the third Chryse core himself, laid a thumbnail across the rust band and the black reduction band beneath it, and entered both depths in the log — two colors, on a planet with no business laying down even one.

**Ending directive** — An irreversible act of the hands at the terraforming controls — a valve, a firing order, a switch completed or refused — that answers whether he finished the world or began to burn it; the verdict legible in the act alone, never spoken, the aftermath never shown.

**Forbidden**
- The waking-god reveal: the aquifer remnant given a face, a will, an intent to attack. It stays biochemistry and ecology, a condition read through instruments — never rendered, never met.
- Precursor-alien lore: the first terraformers as a named civilization with ruins, glyphs, a plaque, a message left for finders. They are legible only through geochemistry and geomorphology; no artifact, no warning marker (that is Nº 31).
- The panspermia loop: the predecessors turn out to be humans, or us, or Earth's own origin — the cheap twist that collapses inheritance into identity.
- Outbreak thriller: the Martian biology fought or quarantined scene by scene, patient-zero epidemiology (that is Nº 41).
- Eden sentimentality and clean sacrifice: the new biosphere as innocent paradise defiled, the moral resolved as hubris punished or nature vindicated, sterilization played as a heroic clean cut. Either choice murders a world; keep the cost total and the hands dirty.

**Levers**
- (a) *Structure:* collapse to single POV on Chaudhary for a tighter builder's tragedy; or braid in the first Eden as a forward deep-time strand (its own director, its own last day) — but that strand courts Nº 39 *Lagerstätte* and must be fenced from reconstructed-alien narrative.
- (b) *Darker/warmer:* warmer — a negotiated third way, a preserved reserve or a covenant to keep the thaw partial; darker — they finish and the book closes before consequence, or they sterilize and it was already loose, or does not take.
- (c) *Scope:* chamber down to Chryse Deep and the sampling breach (a kammerspiel of the reading); or blow up to solar-system politics — the choice fought among Earth agencies and a treaty crisis over who may un-terraform a world with people on it.
