# Nº 13 — Seismograph · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — the debt of inherited safety: someone always paid for the sky staying empty, and this is the generation the bill comes due on — what a person owes when the price of four quiet billion years is payable only in one life she has to name.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 31 *Not a Place of Honor*: both descend into a made thing left by a vanished intelligence and stake lives on reading its message correctly. Split on what the thing *is* — 31's vault is dead, its message a warning built to keep the ignorant *away*, and the novel ends at the threshold with the occupant unshown; *Seismograph*'s machine is *running*, its message a maintenance request that summons the knowing *in*, and the book must cross the threshold and transact. Make it decipherment of a tomb and it is 31. (Nº 14 *The Last Lighthouse*, the instrument-cosmic-horror twin, is split from its side: it never drills and its threat stays a graph; this book drills and pays.)

**The two questions**
- Outward — How does a small far-side crew reach a resonant chamber kilometers down, with a drill that makes meters a day and a tone collapsing toward continuous, before the shield that has taken Earth's impacts for four billion years goes silent?
- Inward — What must a scientist become to read a demand for a human life as a true invoice and not a horror — to accept that survival here was always a debt paid in a person, and to be the one who chooses whom?

**Dyad**
- Equation — a shrinking recurrence interval raced against a borehole. The tone repeats on an 18-day cycle that shortens on a decay curve toward zero (continuous tone = shield silent); the drill advances a fixed meters/day against km of megaregolith and a finite consumable/power budget. You reach and re-key the chamber before the interval closes, or you don't.
- Mirror — Espen Rasmussen, Sarita's driller of twenty years, who reaches the cavity wall first and whom the mechanism couples to: the currency is a living resonator of the right bandwidth, and the descent has already selected the body deepest and best-seated in the rock. His.

**Shape** — 3rd limited, single POV (Sarita Ramanathan), past tense. Chapters headed by the seismic ledger — cycle number, recurrence interval (falling), tone duration, drill depth, meters to chamber, consumable/power margin, next relay window. ~115,000 words, ~36 chapters, three parts: Detection / Descent / Settlement.

**The novum's price**
- Flesh — the payment is a keeper who stays: a nervous system entrained to sustain the tone, consumed slowly and in place, not a clean death.
- Money/mass — the descent eats the station's whole margin; every meter is life-support days spent, and a lost drill string is mass they cannot relaunch to the far side.
- A relationship — the twenty-year partnership ends with Sarita spending Espen (or taking his place), and she carries the ledger of that transaction for the rest of the book.

**Science memo pointers** — Lunar seismology: Apollo Passive Seismic Experiment / ALSEP, deep moonquakes at 700–1200 km tied to the tidal (perigee) cycle, and the Moon's genuinely long reverberant coda — low attenuation, high Q, a body that already "rings" (normal modes, free oscillations). Map the voids by full-waveform inversion; treat the chambers as coupled resonators (Helmholtz / organ-pipe eigenmodes). Gravimetry: superconducting gravimeters, GRAIL-class gravity fields, tidal gravimetry — why an 18-day period matches no natural lunar cycle (the anomalistic month is 27.55 d) and is therefore the tell. Cratering: the size-frequency production function, the ~20:1 Earth/Moon impact-flux ratio by gravitational cross-section, late heavy bombardment — know real rates to make "too many, too precise" bite. Deep drilling: Kola Superdeep (12.26 km), ICDP practice, PDC bits, rotary/percussive tradeoffs, no drilling fluid in vacuum, stuck pipe, lost circulation, hole collapse in breccia, the low lunar geothermal gradient. Ops reality: far-side has no direct Earth line — a Queqiao-class relay with occultation windows that cut comms; 1.28 s light-lag; 14-day night forcing nuclear/RTG power; closed-loop ECLSS with the consumable budget as the real clock.

**Opening image candidate** — At the seismometer pier she wound the night's trace off the drum, walked each deep-quake arrival against the tide table with a fingertip, and stopped where a forty-minute tone stood in a slot four billion years had left empty.

**Ending directive** — Close on Sarita at the log: the payment entered, the recurrence reset to the interval the chamber now owes, the next due-date written as a number for a successor she will never meet — the maker never shown, the meaning carried in the act of accounting and not in any voice from below.

**Forbidden**
- The hollow-Moon reveal — "the Moon is a spaceship / artificial." It is mostly real basalt; only *part* is carved. No Death Star.
- The makers on stage — no ancient astronauts, no hologram exposition, no benevolent space-parents watching over humanity. They are absent; the shield is inherited infrastructure with a bill.
- The tone "translated" into language — no decoded speech, no handshake, no first contact. Meaning stays seismic and procedural: a maintenance request read as engineering.
- The keeper's death as transcendence — no glowing merge with the cosmic. It is a cost: slow, entrained, ugly.
- The descent as action set-piece — the dread is a nominal checklist with one line wrong, not monsters in the dark.

**Levers** — (a) POV/structure: go dual — Sarita topside reading the tone against the descent crew below; or add an Earth-side gravimetry thread showing the ring heard worldwide, politics of who owes the debt. (b) darker/warmer: darker — the payment buys only centuries, making it a hereditary keeper-tax that recurs forever; warmer — the crew finds the chamber can be re-keyed by a permanent stationed *instrument* instead of a life, trading the sacrifice for an unending cost in mass and watts. (c) scope: compress to one 18-day cycle as a countdown thriller, or span Sarita's career from first anomaly to final settlement.
