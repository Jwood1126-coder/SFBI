# Nº 29 — Nightfall Below · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — the dignity and loneliness of refusal: what it costs to stay a self that can still say no, when everyone you loved has already said yes and the yes looks like peace.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 03 *The Greenhouse Variable*. Both: survivors on a dying platform reading what emptied the Earth, building toward a descent. The split to enforce — in *Greenhouse* the world was overwritten (people displaced by an engineered ecology, an author inbound, a thing done to bodies); in *Nightfall* the world walked out (eight billion invited and consenting, the ground empty because everyone said yes). If the emptying ever reads as an agent acting on bodies rather than a summons minds accept, it is *Greenhouse*. Keep the horror in the consent; keep this a small-society book against *Greenhouse*'s solitude.

**The two questions**
- Outward — How do eleven survivors and two sister platforms hold a decaying orbit on six-month rations and one under-producing greenhouse long enough to spend their only reentry craft on the ground truth — a craft that goes down and never comes up?
- Inward — What must a person become to keep the capacity to refuse: to still say no when yes is what eight billion chose, and what the one they love may choose on the way down?

**Dyad**
- Equation — The consumables curve (eleven mouths, a greenhouse covering a fraction, stores depleting to a fixed date) sets the launch-by; the descent is one deorbit, no ascent, irreversible; the answer climbs home only in the minutes of each overhead pass before the ground takes the descender. Two clocks: the ration curve deciding when they go, the pass calendar rationing how much truth gets up.
- Mirror — Signe Rask in orbit, Corin Merrow on the ground, spouses; the loop is the marriage stretched across the ionosphere, and her voice calling Corin by name is the last summons on the un-answered side.

**Shape** — Close third, two POVs: Signe (the anchor who stays; survival engineering, the strain among eleven) and Corin (the descender; the intact, emptied city and the pull of the yes). Past tense. Measured in passes — orbital chapters headed by the standing tally (pass number and ground-track, days-of-air, days-of-water, greenhouse yield, crew count); ground chapters by mission-elapsed time and minutes-to-next-window. Parts: *the dark* / *the federation* / *the descent*. ~110,000 words, ~34 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Mass/irreversibility — the descent spends the remnant's only reentry craft: their sole lifeboat, one way off the station ever, plus two people, on an answer they cannot act on — and it never comes back up.
- Flesh/relationship — the yes acts on the descender directly; holding out means running the block, which blunts and isolates whoever wears it, and the bill falls due when the second descender takes it off and walks into the yes on the open loop.
- Others' lives — mounting the descent (checkouts, propellant transfer, launch-day power, keeping Corin's loop pointed) is subtracted from the nine who stay; every hour of prep is days of air off their curve. Learning the truth shortens the lives of the people kept alive to hear it.

**Science memo pointers** — Closed-loop ECLSS: CO₂ removal (CDRA sieve beds, LiOH backup, Sabatier), O₂ by OGA electrolysis, water recovery (WPA/UPA); rates ~0.84 kg O₂ / ~3–4 L potable / ~1.8 kg dry food per person-day; ISS failure families (CDRA fouling, OGA/Sabatier faults, coolant ammonia); no loop is fully closed — makeup mass is the killer. Bioregenerative greenhouse: BIOS-3, MELiSSA, ISS Veggie/APH — PPFD, DLI (mol/m²/day), yield per m², harvest index, the honest caloric shortfall (no real space garden feeds a crew). Orbital mechanics: LEO ~400 km, ~92-min period, ground-track/revisit geometry (minutes overhead per pass; global coverage needs many orbits — the "between one pass and the next" mechanism), drag decay without reboost (months–2 yr, F10.7/Kp), CMG desaturation. Descent: deorbit Δv ~100 m/s, entry interface ~120 km, corridor, peak heating and g-load. LOAD-BEARING WALL: surface-to-LEO is ~9.4 km/s Δv — a small reentry craft cannot return to orbit; resolve before drafting via a one-way descent (honest) or a rigorously justified ascent (a still-fueled ground stack). Comms: LEO pass duration ~5–10 min, Doppler, S/X-band link budgets — truth arrives in fragments. Act-I remote-sensing forensics: telescope GSD vs. aperture at 400 km, thermal-IR waste-heat of cooling cities, VIIRS Day-Night Band radiometry, photometric change-detection — ruling out war (no blast/EMP), impact (no crater/ejecta/dust veil), pandemic (bodies would remain; none do), Carrington/CME (fries electronics, not streets). Human factors: LEO dose without ground forecasting (SPE/GCR), indefinite-confinement psychology; ground the block in real sedation/attention literature. The one implausibility is the summons — spend it there, and pin *why orbit is out of its reach* (a vector needing ground-level exposure, attenuated by atmospheric path or the planet's mass/field) so it survives an engineer, even unexplained.

**Opening image candidate** — On the third pass of the watch, Signe pulled the spent scrubber bed, weighed it against the manifest that would not be replenished, and marked one more canister off a count that only went down.

**Ending directive** — Close in orbit, not below, on Signe's hand finishing the day's count — a canister logged, a hatch dogged, a reading taken — the gesture the book opened on, now carrying the choice to remain unanswered. Whether Corin's voice is still on the loop, and whether anything below is still calling, lives in the gesture: no speech that names or accepts the yes, no witnessed departure, no far side shown.

**Forbidden**
- The Rapture literalized — beams, ascension, a benevolent shepherd/god/AI shown gathering people up; the departure is never spectacle and never has a face.
- The yes explained — a found recording, a lucid survivor, an intelligence that narrates where everyone went; it stays inference from evidence, and the book ends before the far side.
- The empty-city sentimental tour — abandoned-mall melancholy, dust and a lonely dog, looters, spray-painted scripture; the clocks are still running, so use that, and keep the ground instrument-eyed, not tourism.
- A human holdout below who resisted and can explain — a colony that "didn't answer" breaks the premise that everyone consented; the only un-summoned are in orbit.
- The orbital half as bottle-episode soap (mutiny, love triangles) while the ration math goes soft; scarcity stays real arithmetic, and the dread is a nominal checklist with one line wrong.

**Levers** — (a) Collapse to Signe's single orbital POV, the ground known only through loop and telescope (the *Lighthouse* move), so the descent is pure inference; or invert to Corin's single ground POV, the station a voice. (b) Darker: both descenders yield and the loop cuts out mid-report, so the remnant must stay without ever knowing; warmer: Corin resists and the book becomes whether return is even possible (a fueled ground stack), refusal flown back up. (c) Contract to the descent alone, near-real-time across a handful of passes; or widen to the three-platform federation as a governing body, the descent a contested vote over whether to spend the last vehicle at all.
