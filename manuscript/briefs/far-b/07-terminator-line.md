# Nº 07 — Terminator Line · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — how a decent people talks itself, one defensible step at a time, into leaving its weak behind, and what the crossing makes of the one who keeps the ledger that prices them.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 09 *Blackbody*. Both are survival on a thermal-gradient world, both a migration toward heat. Enforce the empty-furnace split: in *Blackbody* the migration, the mind, and the negotiation belong to an infrared xenobiology and warmth is inhabited; here the dayside is dead physics, the sun a parameter, the only "other" the caravan's own conscience. Any chapter that negotiates with the environment or finds life in the heat belongs to the other book.

**The two questions**
- Outward — How do eleven thousand walk months into a dayside furnace — gradient climbing a degree per kilometer, water finite, the freeze-out sealing the ring behind them — to a redoubt built for fewer than set out?
- Inward — What must the woman who sets each march and rations the column become, to keep pricing lives against distance and stay someone the survivors can bear to have followed?

**Dyad**
- Equation — a closing corridor between two fronts: the nightside collapse depressurizing the ring behind, the dayside superheating ahead as thinning air fails to shed heat off the substellar point. Fixed distance to the Forge; a survivable wet-bulb band migrating dayward faster than the column can walk; water at 6–10 L/person/day and falling. Every day on the slow raises the temperature the fast arrive into.
- Mirror — Tobias Aldis, her father, the founding route-surveyor who taught her this ground, now cataract-blind and slow: the man whose wager doomed them, and by her own manifest's rule the passenger the crossing can least afford.

**Shape** — Close third, single POV (Sabra Aldis), past tense. Chapters headed by the day's crossing ledger — days out, km to the Forge, ambient °C (rising), pressure in mbar (falling), water per person, reconciled head-count (falling) — the count line the only place the abandonment is ever named. ~120,000 words, ~36 chapters.

**The novum's price**
- Mass/water — a body that cannot walk must be hauled, and hauled mass is liters and calories off the shared margin; she prices her father, by name, in the ledger she uses for strangers.
- Flesh — the dayward gradient drives wet-bulb past survivable; the crossing is paid in overheated bodies (exertional heatstroke, rhabdomyolysis, renal shutdown), and the strongest haulers burn first.
- Time/relationship — every hour held for the rear raises the arrival temperature for the front; mercy behind is subtracted from lives ahead, and she does the subtraction aloud.

**Science memo pointers** — Tidally-locked M-dwarf climate: 3D GCMs (ExoCAM, ROCKE-3D, LMD-G), substellar convection, day–night heat redistribution, terminator-ring habitability, and the load-bearing failure mode — nightside cold-trapping and volatile freeze-out (Wordsworth), with the runaway where thinning atmosphere weakens advection and superheats the day. Star: Gliese 887 (GJ 887), M2V, ~10.7 ly, unusually flare-quiet; real GJ 887c candidate; N₂/CO₂/H₂O vapor-pressure curves at low T. Thermal physiology: wet-bulb globe temperature and the ~35 °C survivability limit (Sherwood & Huber), sweat rate 1–1.5 L/hr, dehydration vs. hyponatremia, acclimatization, work–rest cycles. Logistics: ration math, load-hauling energetics over grade, historical overland-column mortality (Oregon Trail, the Long Walk, refugee-corridor curves). Substellar geothermal: enhanced-geothermal heat exchange at a hot ocean, sizing the Forge. Instruments: sling psychrometer / WBGT meter, aneroid barometer-altimeter, IR pyrometer, UV dosimeter; navigation under a fixed sun — solar elevation the only latitude, celestial nav impossible, dead-reckoning and landmark survey.

**Opening image candidate** — At the head of the column she spun the psychrometer until the wet-bulb held, chalked the reading against the day's kilometer stake, and struck two more names from the water manifest before the haulers finished loading.

**Ending directive** — Close on the manifest's last reconciliation — the count that crossed against the berths the Forge actually holds — resolved by one act of her hand: a name entered, struck, or the ledger closed unfinished. Whether the crossing has made the caravan into something that will cull again or refuse to must live in the gesture, never in speech.

**Forbidden**
- The dayside inhabited: any nonhuman mind, negotiated warmth, or thing living in the heat — that is *Blackbody*; the furnace stays empty physics.
- Atrocity spectacle — cannibalism, a caravan tyrant, a mob murdering the weak; the dread is that the triage stays quiet and reasonable, argued by decent, correct people.
- The heat-madness montage — hallucination, delirium, competence dissolving; the arithmetic stays clear-eyed to the last page.
- The uplift arrival or the total wipe; the Forge's cull is a standing, unresolved cost, answered by neither everyone-lives nor everyone-dies.
- The fixed sun as verdict-poetry; it is a navigational and thermal parameter first, its elevation their only latitude.

**Levers** — (a) Interleave Tobias's original habitability survey, in his surveyor's hand, as found-text interchapters against the crossing it caused. (b) Darker: the Forge is already lost or held by an earlier splinter column at arrival, and the book becomes what happens past the finish line; warmer: a second survivable niche found mid-crossing reframes the exodus as choice, not sentence. (c) Scope: compress to a single leg between two water caches, near-real-time, for clock — or widen across generations, founding survey to long after, for meaning.
