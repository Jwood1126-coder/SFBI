# Nº 20 — Slow Wave · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — the end of solitude: when the last private room — your own sleep — becomes a public square every human stands in nightly, what it costs to keep a self, and who pays to be the one locked out.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 14 *The Last Lighthouse*, the instrument-cosmic-horror twin. Split on where the threat lives. Lighthouse keeps it wholly external and astronomical — inferred at a distance, the monster stays a graph and never touches the human sphere; no ship launches, no contact. *Slow Wave* inverts this: the threat is already inside every skull from chapter one — the instrument is the sleeping brain, not a telescope, and the arena is the shared interior, felt and social and dreamed by billions at once. Enforce: never let the drama migrate to an astronomy desk. Its instruments are polysomnography and dream-content coding; its territory is the field everyone is standing in. The moment the exoplanet becomes the arena, it is Lighthouse.

**The two questions**
- Outward — How do people keep grids up, hospitals staffed, and children fed when sleep stops being rest and becomes a nightly commute the whole species takes to the same darkening field?
- Inward — What must a scientist become to map a country she is standing inside — to keep an outside, an observer's distance, when there is no longer any outside left to observe from?

**Dyad**
- Equation — the convergence curve. The dream's resolution, measured as a residual against the team's reconstructed sky, sharpens on a fittable nightly decay; the figure's range closes in dream-strides per night. The team extrapolates a completion date with tightening confidence intervals — a countdown you can compute to the day and cannot stop.
- Mirror — Salma and Idris, her spouse, one of the sleepless. He cannot enter the dream, so the new universal intimacy is the one thing she cannot bring home: she now shares her interior with every stranger on Earth and, with him, nothing.

**Shape** — 3rd limited, dual POV (Salma inside the concordance / Idris outside it, the exile), past tense. Chapters headed by the nightly resolution log — night number, sky-residual (falling), figure range in strides (falling), global concordance index, mean sleep-debt/impairment, next survey window. ~120,000 words, ~34 chapters, three parts: Onset / Territory / Completion.

**The novum's price**
- Flesh & time — REM is spent downloading, not restoring; the species runs on the neurophysiology of chronic deprivation (immune suppression, glucose dysregulation, microsleeps, psychosis at the tail). Salma is not exempt from her own subject — her errors mount as her own sleep stops repairing her.
- A relationship — Idris's sleeplessness makes him a priceless control and an unreachable spouse; she studies the one mind she loves and cannot visit, and what she newly shares with every stranger alive is the one thing she can never share with him.
- Money & labor — the sleepless are the only unimpaired workers left; a rationed caste economy forms around them, and Salma must spend budget, favors, or Idris himself to keep either the lab or the marriage running.

**Science memo pointers** — Sleep medicine and polysomnography: the AASM montage (EEG/EOG/EMG), sleep staging, slow-wave sleep (N3, 0.5–4 Hz delta — the title), REM architecture, sawtooth and PGO waves, REM atonia, electrode impedance <5 kΩ. Objective dream scoring: the Hall–Van de Castle content-analysis system and inter-rater reliability — the only real instrument that could prove two reports are the *same*, not merely alike; LaBerge-style lucid-dream eye-movement signaling as the real basis for two-way contact within REM. Deprivation physiology: total-sleep-deprivation studies, microsleeps, the 17–19 h awake ≈ 0.05% BAC figure, and fatal familial insomnia (prion, PRNP D178N, thalamic degeneration) as the real face of the sleepless. Inter-brain synchrony / hyperscanning as the seed literature for "REM synchronization." The handshake astronomy, held hard: direct imaging of exoplanets (coronagraphs, starshades, angular differential imaging) and why "40 ly, imaged three years ago" sits at the physical edge — pick a real host star from the Gaia catalog so the constellation-match from that vantage is a genuine parallax/proper-motion calculation, and decide explicitly whether the return packet honors light-time (a process long en route, the imaging merely its trigger) or spends the book's one implausibility on nonlocality — everything else stays rigorous. Public-health operations: error-rate epidemiology, grid/aviation/hospital function under universal impairment, and the surveillance sampling and signal-detection statistics a species-wide phenomenon would actually require.

**Opening image candidate** — She worked the gel under the last electrode until the impedance meter fell below five kilohms, keyed the channel live, and wrote the time before she looked at the subject's face at all.

**Ending directive** — An act at the threshold between the dreaming and the one who cannot dream — a thing done, logged, or reached across the line between Salma and Idris — rendered as procedure. What arrived when the download completed stays entirely off the page; end on the human gesture at the divide, not on the content of the field.

**Forbidden**
- The dream as lush surrealism — the field must be read the way a technician reads a record: flat, measured, the resolution logged. No dream-logic set pieces; no adjective carrying the awe.
- The walker shown, or its message decoded into language — no face, no body, no sentence in English. The moment the occupant of the field is rendered, the book dies.
- The dreamer-messiah — Salma as the one special mind with privileged access. She is a technician, not a prophet; the phenomenon is flatly universal and she has no private line to it.
- The war-room thriller — no plan to bomb the exoplanet, no "seal the borders of sleep." Institutions arrive as memos, rationing, and protocol.
- Shared-dream-as-allegory — no sermon equating it with the internet, social media, or a Jungian collective unconscious. Keep it a measured physical phenomenon, not a metaphor with a moral.

**Levers** — (a) POV/structure: go single on Idris — the whole book from outside the commons, the dream only ever secondhand; or run dream-report interludes as an epidemiological chorus. (b) darker/warmer: make Idris's sleeplessness literal fatal familial insomnia, so exile from the commons is also a death sentence (darker); or let the sleepless discover they can carry messages between waking and the field, trading exile for a fragile usefulness (warmer). (c) scope: compress to the original two subjects and one lab, the species-wide event kept offstage as news and numbers; or span a generation, until children born native to the shared dream outnumber those who remember private sleep.
