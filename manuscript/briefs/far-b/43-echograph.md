# Nº 43 — Echograph · FAR (Tier B)

1. **Secretly about** — the last freedom under a fixed outcome: a man spends himself trying to avert a catastrophe he can read but cannot change, and learns that determinism leaves exactly one free act — not prevention but answer — so that becoming the older voice on the feed means forgiving, in advance, the choice to let it happen.

2. **Sibling rule** — Nº 42 *Attenuation*: both are radio-reception verification procedurals whose signal proves addressed to the protagonist. Enforce the split 42's brief already canonizes — *Echograph* receives **exogenous** information Earth does not possess (its own future, from a distant sender) and runs on **determinism**, a fixed self-consistent record; *Attenuation* receives only Earth's recombined **past** and runs on answerability-at-a-cost. The instant Echograph's feed becomes changeable, the books collapse. (Also fence Nº 45 *Aoide* — the impossible voice on the receiver — by staying temporal/deterministic where 45 is spatial/relativistic salvage; and Nº 13 *Seismograph*, the other far-side observatory, held apart by siting in **Korolev crater**, not Daedalus.)

3. **The two questions**
- Outward — How does a small, isolated far-side crew verify a physically impossible signal past every hoax, artifact, and RFI explanation, then — unable to alter one dated event — build the reply apparatus the final fragment specifies before the record runs to silence?
- Inward — What must Asa become to stop trying to save a future he cannot change and instead answer it: to accept authorship of the older voice on the feed, and choose, knowing, to let the unchangeable happen while doing the one thing left that is his to do?

4. **Dyad**
- Equation — the timestamp-and-light-cone clamp. Apparent source ~40 ly; content self-dated ~30 years ahead (a true mirror would return our *past*, not our future — the tell); any reply crosses 40 years one-way, so nothing outruns the signal; and every intervention leaves the ongoing record unchanged (Novikov self-consistency). The budget is the shrinking **lead** — the closing gap between the array's real present and the dated final fragment — against finite consumables and relay windows to finish the reply before that date arrives. A fixed record you race to *complete*, not to break.
- Mirror — Asa and **Reyna Sandoval**, co-leads and the two voices heard, older, on the future feed. The bond is what determinism tests: they must become the selves the feed already showed — the ones who let it happen — without letting it end them; and neither can save the person of Asa's whom the dated casualty data names.

5. **Shape** — Single close-third, Asa Nakamura only (the airtight POV is the discipline), past tense — with the received broadcasts rendered in **present tense**, so the future arrives on the page as a now. Chapters headed by the reception ledger: array date, the fragment's self-timestamp, the lead (falling), measured source distance/dispersion, verification status, next relay window. "RECEIVED" transcript interchapters carry the dated newscasts, provenance on the page. ~120,000 words, ~34 chapters, three parts: Reception / The Fixed Record / The Answer.

6. **The novum's price**
- Flesh/relationship — Asa's daughter on Earth is named in a dated evacuation fragment; the 40-ly geometry and self-consistency mean he can neither warn her nor change it. He spends months building a warning the record proves useless, and reads the date toward him.
- Mass/money/time — verification and the reply build eat the station's whole margin: every intervention — re-pointing the array, cutting a relay to warn Earth — is instrument-time and consumables spent to move a record that does not move; the apparatus is mass they cannot relaunch, and station-days of air.
- The self/the crew — hearing his own older voice makes Asa its author; a crewmember breaks trying to prove the future mutable, at a cost in a life or the array, and the marriage-of-purpose with Reyna is spent choosing, together, to stop saving and start answering.

7. **Science memo pointers** — Far-side low-frequency radio cosmology: the 21 cm Dark Ages/Cosmic Dawn signal; why the far side (Earth's radio shadow, no ionosphere below the ~10–30 MHz plasma cutoff); real programs FARSIDE, LCRT, DSL, LuSEE-Night, PRATUSH; Earth analogues LOFAR, LWA, MWA, HERA, SKA-Low, and EDGES's disputed absorption as a caution. Array practice: dipole fields, beamforming, interferometry, the u–v plane, calibration, galactic-synchrotron foreground subtraction. Verification culture: RFI excision; why an Earth broadcast on the far side is a screaming artifact flag; the full exclusion — limb leakage, orbiting relays, crew RFI, cable pickup, intermodulation, pipeline artifact, insider hoax, chain of custody on the raw voltages; false-positive history (Wow!, LGM-1, BLC1). Source localization: VLBI/interferometric parallax, dispersion measure, and the energetics proving Earth leakage is undetectable at 40 ly — so the source is a dedicated high-power transmitter, not a reflector. The novum's rigor (spend the one implausibility here): closed timelike curves (Gödel, Tipler, Morris–Thorne–Yurtsever); the Novikov self-consistency principle as the honest engine of the determinism; the grandfather and bootstrap paradoxes; Deutsch vs. post-selected CTCs (Lloyd, Aaronson); retrocausality (Wheeler–Feynman absorber theory, Cramer's transactional interpretation, the two-state-vector formalism); Hawking's chronology-protection conjecture as the objection the book knowingly violates exactly once. Ops: 40 ly = 40-year one-way replies; no Earth line (Earth–Moon L2 relay, Queqiao-class, occultation windows, 1.28 s lag); the 14-day night forcing RTG/nuclear power; closed-loop ECLSS with the consumable/relay budget as the real clock.

8. **Opening image candidate** — In the shielded midday of the array, Asa ran the night's integration down against the galactic-foreground model and stopped his pencil where a clean terrestrial voice-band stood forty light-years deep — a carrier in the one place built so Earth could not be heard.

9. **Ending directive** — Close on an act of answering rendered as procedure: the built reply apparatus keyed by hand, the carrier raised into the channel, the received feed running down to its silence, and Asa logging that silence as a handover rather than an ending — end on the act or the object, before any sign of what the answer did.

10. **Forbidden**
- The averted future — no clever loophole, no "we changed it after all"; Novikov holds, and the only act that counts completes the loop rather than breaks it. A time-travel adventure kills the book.
- The senders on stage — no benevolent future-humans exposition, no faces or dialogue for the survivors; they exist only as the signal and the final instructions (makers off-stage, per 13/31).
- The physics as the point — no CTC lecture, no paradox puzzle-box or twist-thriller cleverness; the mechanism is one implausibility, the subject is the weight of foreknowledge.
- The feed as ghost-mood — every fragment is a sourced, dated, specific newscast doing informational work, never atmosphere, never "translated" into portent.
- The own-voice as gotcha — Asa's older voice is a moral assignment, not a whodunit hook; don't milk "whose voice is it" for suspense. (And don't drift into Nº 14 *The Last Lighthouse*'s predator-from-shadows procedural, or a first-contact handshake.)

11. **Levers** — (a) POV/structure: go dual (Asa + Reyna), or present-tense throughout to enact the fixed now, or braid an Earth-side thread living the dated events forward. (b) darker/warmer: darker — a pure bootstrap, the reply's only effect to make the senders exist, meaning without rescue; warmer — the reply demonstrably lets the far survivors prepare, anticipatory grief turned into a gift sent forward. (c) scope: compress to the locked station and the verify-to-answer arc (airtight), or widen to Earth-side classification politics and a world reacting to proof of its own dated end.
