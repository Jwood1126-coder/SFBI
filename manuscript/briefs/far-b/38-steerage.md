# Nº 38 — Steerage · FAR (Tier B)

**Secretly about** — that to count the helpless is to become the officer at their gate: handed a refugee ship of billions in failing stasis, already triaging its own steerage to stay alive, the reader of the manifest learns that inventory is admission — that to catalogue who lives is to author who dies, and that declining the office is only a quieter way of holding it.

**Sibling rule** — Nº 39 *Lagerstätte*: both hand an exopaleontologist the forensic reconstruction of a vanished civilization from its physical remains, and both wake a dormant artifact on a trigger. Split on what the remains *are* and what the waking summons. *Lagerstätte*'s dead thing is a surveyor — an observer who catalogued us, whose beacon calls a verdict inbound. *Steerage*'s dead things are passengers — cargo, an estate — and nothing answers from outside; the only thing that arrives is the obligation. Let Prosper file a report on humanity, or let anything approach in reply, and the book has become *Lagerstätte* with a bigger hold.

**The two questions**
- Outward — How does an inventory contractor aboard a mining borer keep billions of thousand-year-failing stasis cells viable on a prospector's power-and-heat budget, on a body already falling back toward interstellar space, while custody law, a biotech boarding party, and the ark's own triage all move faster than she can count?
- Inward — What must Yoon become to stop being the neutral cataloguer and accept the executor's authorship: to decide, by her own hand, which passengers die so others live, and so become the ark's heir rather than its auditor.

**Dyad**
- Equation — the stasis load-shed: viable cells sustained versus the borer's spare power and cooling, against a decay curve a thousand years steepening and a hard departure window as 3I/Prosper recedes from the Sun — every watt spent to hold one cargo class struck from another, the triage made the crew's to run.
- Mirror — Yoon and the steward that came out of the flagged container: the thing that has run the admission alone since before human writing, now failing with its ship, handing over the arithmetic the way a dying parent hands over a debt — with love, and without leaving her the choice to decline.

**Shape** — Single close third on Yoon, past tense. The manifest is the structural device: chapters headed by one cargo line in the builders' notation with her running gloss and a disposition column — HELD / TRIAGED / FAILED — that fills, over the book, in her own hand rather than the ark's. Three parts by the customs of an estate she is forced to keep: the Claim / the Inventory / the Office. ~130,000 words, ~36 chapters. (Distinct from Nº 35's necrology: this ledger rolls passengers she chooses among, not worlds an indifferent fleet consumes.)

**The novum's price**
- Mass and money — holding the load burns the borer's return margin: reactor-hours and propellant spent on cargo instead of departure, and the crew's shares in a trillion-dollar volatiles claim go worthless the hour the manifest is believed and the ice becomes people.
- Flesh — the triage is not automated for her; to keep the viable fraction alive on a shrinking budget she decants and lets specific lineages fail by hand, thousands of them, choosing off a screen and then doing the physical shut-off — and the opened container needs a living substrate, so quarantine breaks and carrying the duty costs a crew body.
- A relationship — comms lag to Earth climbs with the range, every custody ruling hours stale before it lands; and the one person on the biotech side she trusts, sent to take the genomes, becomes the person she must seal out — the friendship spent to hold the gate.

**Science memo pointers** — Interstellar objects: 1I/ʻOumuamua and 2I/Borisov, hyperbolic excess velocity, non-gravitational acceleration (the outgassing debate), detection by Pan-STARRS and the expected ISO haul from Vera Rubin / LSST; the intercept problem (Project Lyra, solar-Oberth and powered-flyby Δv, tens-of-km/s encounter speeds, the short window, why you cannot easily stop at one). Comet-nucleus structure and ices (H₂O, CO, CO₂, CH₄, NH₃), crust/mantle, borer mining and outgassing. Cryobiology done honestly: vitrification, cryoprotectants (DMSO, glycerol, trehalose), devitrification and chilling injury as failure modes; anhydrobiosis (tardigrades, brine-shrimp cysts, rotifers), radiation-tolerant life (*Deinococcus*); and the deep-time information limit — depurination and racemization rates, cosmic-ray dose over a millennium, DNA repair — which grounds "quietly failing." Preservation as optimization: minimum viable population and effective population size (the 50/500 rule), inbreeding depression and drift, and Weitzman's "Noah's Ark Problem" — the triage is real conservation-prioritization math, not a whim. Thermal and power engineering: cryocooler work against the Carnot limit, radiator heat rejection in vacuum, reactor budget far from the Sun. Biobanking: Svalbard, germplasm accession, viability testing, IVF embryo and oocyte cryopreservation. Law: Outer Space Treaty and "province of all mankind," the absence of a space-salvage regime, COSPAR planetary protection and quarantine for the opened container, the Nagoya Protocol / biopiracy frame for the genomes. Name instrument-systematics-read-as-signal and over-attribution (Dennett's intentional stance and its failure mode) as standing hazards.

**Opening image candidate** — Yoon signed off the night's cores as commercial ice, forty of them, until the assay returned the forty-first ruled into cells too even to be ice, and she set down the stylus and began, out of a habit older than the contract, to count them.

**Ending directive** — Close on Yoon setting a disposition on the manifest in her own hand — one line moved between columns by a human decision — the office accepted or declined in the act of writing and never in a statement of it; the builders' duty neither named in full nor discharged aloud, and no answer from the dark that it was received.

**Forbidden**
- The answering fleet — a beacon that phones home and something arrives; nothing comes, and the only arrival is the obligation.
- The reason for the voyage captioned — the duty explained in a builder's recorded message or a reveal monologue; it stays enacted and priced, never captioned.
- The waking-god in the hold — the opened container's contents as a creature hunting the crew; it is a passenger and an office, and the horror is a viability curve and a quarantine breach, not a beast in the vents.
- The biotech-villain caper — corporate baddies versus noble scientist, espionage set pieces; the antagonist is the arithmetic and the law, and every faction holds a legible claim.
- Us-as-chosen — the ark "meant" to find us; it was aimed at nothing, our finding it is contingent, and inheritance-without-being-chosen is the whole point.

**Levers** — (a) braid in the ark's own thousand-year triage log as interchapters, the builders' system reconstructed decision by decision, versus staying wholly in Yoon's present — guard the braid against Nº 39's two-timeline shape. (b) Darker/warmer: darker — Yoon runs the admission, saves a fraction, and the book is what it makes of a woman to turn most of a species away; warmer — the duty proves shareable, humanity is inducted as co-steward, and the ark's solitude ends. (c) Scope: compress to a shipboard chamber piece — one crew, one shift-cycle, the boarding party at the airlock — versus widening to the decade-long custody war across Earth's institutions with Yoon one node in it.

**Registry** — *Dr. Sam Yoon* (protagonist; exopaleontologist, the manifest-reader) — grep-clean, only the dossier and an unrelated "Sam Cooke" record (ch01) in the corpus; no protagonist or POV named Sam elsewhere. *3I/Prosper* (the interstellar object / ark) — clean, dossier only. *Capstan* (the volatiles consortium that wins the intercept) and the borer *Longshore* (Yoon's ship) — both 0 corpus hits, maritime-salvage naming chosen to hold the steerage frame; two new proper nouns, within the far-voice budget. The steward from the flagged container is left unregistered — a role-noun ("the steward," "the caretaker") in prose, to avoid fixing content or granting it a face. No renames; the dossier's given name and object designation both survive the grep.
