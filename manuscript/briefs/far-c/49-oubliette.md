# Nº 49 — Oubliette · FAR (Tier C)

0. **The repair** — The flaw is structural: the book withholds its central fact — Anchorite is a containment site, the inmates are the garrison's descendants — to Act III, which violates the clarity law and collapses into Nº 31 *Not a Place of Honor* (a buried hazard, a warning built to outlast its makers, a descent ending at a threshold). The fix: move the inversion to the first-act break. Spend the withheld thing not on *what is buried* but on *whether free people, told their guilt was a retention trick, will man the wall the trick was built to man*.

1. **Secretly about** — Inherited guilt as load-bearing infrastructure: what people become when you remove the lie holding them up, and whether a duty can be re-chosen once you know no one is owed it.

2. **Sibling rule** — Nº 31 *Not a Place of Honor*. Both bury a hazard behind a message built to outlast its makers. The split: Nº 31 descends to decode a warning and ends at the threshold, mystery intact; Oubliette is the *ascent back to the population* after the fact is known, its subject a living society re-consenting. The instant this becomes about deciphering what was buried, or ends at a sealed door, it is Nº 31.

3. **The two questions** — *Outward:* how does a stranded caste-society keep three kilometres of ice cold, braced, and pumped — the containment its penal quota secretly *is* — when the quake has started the ice failing and the truth has started the workforce failing? *Inward:* what must Joss become to offer his people a wall to man, knowing the guilt that manned it was fabricated, and that the offer costs more than the lie ever did?

4. **Dyad** — *Equation:* a coupled deadline — heat ingress and fracture from the quake driving the deep-ice temperature toward the threshold where containment fails, against the labour-hours of refreeze and bracing needed to re-seal it, hours a de-motivated population will no longer supply. *Mirror:* Joss (miner) and Mirren (warden), who share a child the colony's law will move to the administrative levels at a fixed age — the fiction made personal, the caste line drawn through one family.

5. **Shape** — Dual close third (Joss below, Mirren in the administrative levels), past tense. Chapters headed by the shift ledger: the day's tonnage beside the deep-ice temperature and fissure width — one column read two ways, a sentence served and a wall's vital sign. Four parts: *Sentence · Fissure · Archive · Consent*. ~125,000 words, ~34 chapters.

6. **The novum's price**
   - *Flesh:* the mining *is* the maintenance; keeping the wall spends bodies in the cold and pressurized shafts — cold injury, dust lung, ground-fall — for work owed to no one.
   - *Relationship:* the lie was the glue between castes and inside one family; telling it to save the wall detonates the peace it justified, and may cost the bond and the child both.
   - *Money:* refreeze yields no ore, so diverting reactor heat and labour to containment means famine-level scarcity — choosing the wall is choosing hunger.

7. **Science memo pointers** — Glaciology of a thick icy-satellite shell: conductive/convective thermal profiles, the brittle-ductile transition, hydrofracture, tidally driven icequake seismology (InSight methods, Europa/Enceladus-class flexing). Deep-mine engineering: geothermal gradient, ground support and burst, ventilation, pressurized-shaft decompression physiology. Closed reactor economy: district heat, fission/RTG power, sealed-habitat life support and its failure modes. Isolation by depth and time — deep-repository practice (WIPP) for the *physics* of a barrier, not the marker/message discourse (Nº 31's). Keep the hazard abstract but give it one hard containment law (threshold temperature, fracture-permeability limit). Isolated-population demography, penal-labour economics.

8. **Opening image candidate** — Joss set the resistance probe into the new face, waited for the reading to settle, and chalked the depth-temperature beside the shift's tonnage in the column that had counted, for five generations, as time served.

9. **Ending directive** — An act of chosen labour recorded in the ledger — the same column, now meaning maintenance — or a line in which the wall is offered and not commanded; never a speech that dissolves the guilt, never the buried thing revealed.

10. **Forbidden**
    - The buried thing shown, named, or given a monster's agency or a waking-god stir.
    - The withheld-to-the-end reveal as gotcha; the containment fact is given by the first-act break.
    - Cartoon-jailer wardens and a tyranny-and-uprising plot — no one is in charge; the law enforces itself and the warden-caste is as trapped as the miners.
    - Rescue: no transport returns, no outside authority arrives.
    - The reveal played as liberation — the tragedy is that the lie was structural.

11. **Levers** — (a) Collapse to single POV (Joss), or add a founder-era garrison interchapter — at the cost of the "no living soul knows" purity. (b) Darker: the population refuses the wall and containment fails, an elegy; warmer: a small crew re-consents and the book ends on the first freely-worked shift. (c) Chamber it to the weeks of the fissure and one shaft, or widen to the moon's political economy as the caste order dissolves.

12. **Registry** — **Joss Ratay** (protagonist, miner) and **Anchorite** (the containment moon) — dossier-only corpus-wide, clean. **Mirren Sarn** (co-POV, warden) — 0 hits both names, settled. No vessel named (transports are gone), honouring the noun-tracking rule. Coinage budget (§G, ≤3) near zero: plain **miners** / **wardens**; the fabricated penal doctrine is **the Charge** (accusation and garrison-duty in one word); two reserved. No renames.
