# Nº 27 — Fruiting Bodies · FAR (Tier C)

0. **The repair** — The dossier stakes the whole novel on a third-act reveal ("humans were cultivated; the fruiting is a harvest"), which fails twice: the farmed-species twist is a worn SF gasp, and once sprung it ends the book — nothing drives a second and third act but the withheld secret, and the project's clarity law bans mystery-of-fact. It also collides head-on with Tier A Nº11 Bathypelagic, the other "scientists find a biological substrate computing" book. Repair: promote the cultivation to page one. The Midden already knows, as settled doctrine, that the Thallus gardens them; the sporewrights are its literate caste. The engine is then not discovery but husbandry under an asymmetric partner — the harvest is a scheduled succession the settlement must decide whether to consent to. That converts reveal-thriller to negotiation novel and walls it off from Bathypelagic's illegible, indifferent computation.

1. **Secretly about** — Whether a people can accept a life without primacy — being kept, and answered, and loved the way a gardener loves a crop — and still call it their own.

2. **Sibling rule** — Nº11 Bathypelagic. Enforce the inversion: Bathypelagic's computation never legibly notices the humans (indifference is its horror); the Thallus notices them completely and has shaped them (domestication is ours). The instant Fruiting Bodies asks "does it even know we're here," it has become the other book.

3. **The two questions** — Outward: in a saprotroph world where every crop, cure, and safe winter is an output of the Thallus, how do people stay legible and useful to the thing that provisions them as its own light returns to kill it? Inward: what must they surrender to be a good symbiont — can partnership without being the protagonist be a life, or only a well-fed captivity?

4. **Dyad** — Equation: two curves crossing — stratospheric aerosol optical depth falling (the sky clearing after forty years) against soil necromass drawing down; where returning sunlight lets photoautotrophs reboot, the fungal empire dies, and the Thallus must fruit its human vector before that date. Mirror: Enna and Tarn, the apprentice the Thallus's own fruitings keep selecting over her — the child she is training and the child it means to carry it forward.

5. **Shape** — Single close third, Enna. Past tense. Device: four parts are four fruitings of one cycle; each chapter heads with a line from her field ledger — a Thallus-state reading (optical depth, decay constant k, a signal statistic, a spore count) — the equation ticking under the human scenes. ~110,000 words, ~32 chapters.

6. **The novum's price**
- Flesh: reading and querying the Thallus requires self-inoculation; a sporewright's literacy is a controlled, spreading mycosis that will eventually fruit her — every deeper question costs colonized tissue.
- Food/time: each query draws down the network's finite metabolic budget, so a season of asking is a season of thin rations — knowledge paid in calories the Midden does not eat.
- The dead / a bond: to sustain the exchange the Midden must render its dead into the Thallus rather than bury them, and the Thallus's clearest gift is Tarn, whom it prefers — accepting help means surrendering both the dead and the child.

7. **Science memo pointers** — Mycology: saprotrophic vs. mycorrhizal fungi, hyphal foraging, basidiomycete fruiting-body morphogenesis and its turgor/water-potential hydraulics; white-rot ligninolysis (laccase, manganese peroxidase). The common-mycorrhizal-network "wood-wide web" must be handled at the level of the live scientific fight (Simard's claims vs. the Karst/Jones/Kiers 2023 critique), never the pop version. Fungal electrical spiking (Adamatzky) and VOC signaling — with their caveats. Information theory: how one actually argues "computation" from a signal — mutual information, transfer entropy, tests against a stochastic null; pareidolia as the central failure mode. Instruments: soil microelectrode arrays, in-situ gas chromatography for volatiles, spore-print and culture assay as a survivor's field kit. Decomposition ecology: necromass stocks, C:N ratios, decay constant k, standing-dead drawdown after a mass forest kill. Atmospheric: nuclear-winter/autumn soot injection, stratospheric aerosol optical depth and residence time, radiative forcing (Robock/Toon/Turco); the return of PAR and saprotroph-vs-photoautotroph succession — with an honest justification of the forty-year timescale. Host colonization: Ophiocordyceps behavioral manipulation (real in insects; do not over-claim for mammals), chronic-mycosis immunology.

8. **Opening image candidate** — Enna set the blade where the cap met the ruin's brick and lifted the fruiting body out whole, gills up, to count what the Thallus had spelled that season against the ledger.

9. **Ending directive** — End on an act of Enna's trade — a spore-print laid down, an inoculation taken or refused — that commits the Midden one way; the gesture is the ending, the network's verdict withheld and her reading of it unspoken.

10. **Forbidden**
- The benevolent sentient forest: the Thallus as wise, loving Gaia. It metabolizes; it holds no more tenderness for the Midden than for lignin.
- The restored reveal: any version where "we were farmed" is sprung as a late shock rather than assumed from the first page.
- Mushroom psychedelia: the interface as hallucinatory communion, oneness, colors — the drug-trip scene.
- Pop "trees talk" sentimentality: the network rendered as settled wood-wide-web fact rather than contested inference read by a professional.
- Victory or rapture: humans defeating or escaping the Thallus, or merging with it as transcendence — both cheat the husbandry; the end is terms, not triumph or ascension.

11. **Levers** — (a) Second POV in Tarn, or interchapters that decode the Thallus's own signal-logs as data. (b) Darker: the harvest is composting, not dispersal — the Thallus eats its crop to replace the spent necromass, and the negotiation is over who is rendered. (c) Scope: contract to one fruiting season and the Midden's single vote (chamber piece), or widen to several settlements finding each was a separate cultivar with incompatible answers.

12. **Registry** — Enna Halvorsen (protagonist) — clean; only Vol I Nº27 uses "Enna," and "Halvorsen" repeats solely with Vol II Nº64 (Marit), already a logged rule-4 exception. The Thallus (the network) — 0 corpus hits. Tarn (apprentice / chosen vector) — 0 hits. The Midden (settlement) — 0 hits as a proper noun. No vessel; Earth-bound. Sole coinage is "sporewright" (from the dossier); Thallus and Midden are real words repurposed as names, so the three-term slang cap is unspent. No renames required.
