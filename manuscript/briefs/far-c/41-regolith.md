# Nº 41 — Regolith · FAR (Tier C)

**0. The repair** — The dossier contradicts itself. Act I sells a biochemistry with "no terrestrial ancestor," pre-DNA, wholly alien — the horror. Act III wins by remembering that "Earth's biosphere has beaten this cousin before, in the Hadean" — which requires shared ancestry and a legible record of the contest. Unrelated-alien cannot also be the cousin we already out-competed. The fix, one change: *one abiogenesis, split by the Moon-forming impact.* Life arose once in the proto-Earth/Theia system; the giant impact flung one branch to the Moon as ejecta, freeze-dried at a metabolism-first, redox-driven, pre-DNA stage, while the surviving branch went on to DNA and oxygen. The bloom is genuinely alien to all modern biochemistry — no genes to sequence — yet is the losing sibling of our own line, so the Hadean redox record is an honest weapon. Not a second genesis: the road not taken in ours.

**1. Secretly about** — A conservator's vow to lose nothing meets a world where saving everything is fatal; the book is triage — what a keeper of the dead must be willing to burn to stay on the side of the living.

**2. Sibling rule** — Nº 36 *Second Eden*. Both wake an ancient, sterilized biosphere to the smell of water. Keep them apart by venue and kind: *Second Eden* owns the planetary dig and a prior world's own life; Regolith stays an Earthbound urban containment thriller about the losing twin of *our* biochemistry, never an archaeology.

**3. The two questions** — Outward: how do you extinguish an organism with no genes to target, that eats any terrestrial molecule and out-adapts every protocol, armed only with the chemistry that beat it four billion years ago? Inward: how does a curator become an executioner — learn to destroy the irreplaceable and prefer the living present to the perfect past?

**4. Dyad** — Equation: the bloom's doubling time, measured first in a display case, then a wing, then a city block, raced against how fast the counter-chemistry can be synthesized and delivered — an exponential curve against a supply line. Mirror: Alma, whose trade is to preserve, and Idris Kane, whose trade (planetary protection) is to sterilize — the book's keep-versus-kill argument, ending with the two having traded places.

**5. Shape** — Single close third on Alma (Kane the mirror, never a POV), past tense. Chapters head as conservation condition reports / accession-log entries on the object, the perimeter of concern widening line by line. ~110,000 words, ~40 short chapters.

**6. The novum's price**
- Flesh: a breach seeds a technician; with no clean OR and no time, a colonized forearm comes off in the containment tent — the organism does not wait for surgery.
- Heritage: the counter-ecology takes the substrate with it — ending the bloom means incinerating the pristine Apollo cores, the museum wing, and finally a condemned block of Washington. The irreplaceable burned to save the replaceable.
- Relationship/time: the "find of the century" faction — Alma's own mentor, the science that raised her — must be defeated politically while the curve doubles weekly; the win costs her the mentor and the discipline.

**7. Science memo pointers** — Apollo curation: NASA JSC Lunar Sample Laboratory, nitrogen-glovebox pristine protocols, the sealed Apollo 17 drive tubes and the real ANGSA program that opened core 73001 in 2022 — the vector must be curatorially exact. Astrobiology/planetary protection: COSPAR categories, dry-heat microbial reduction and vaporized-hydrogen-peroxide sterilization, BSL-4 negative-pressure containment and its real failure modes; the shadow-biosphere hypothesis (Davies, Cleland) and the GFAJ-1 "arsenic life" refutation as the cautionary tale for the identification scenes. Origin-of-life geochemistry: giant-impact/Theia Moon formation, Hadean redox, alkaline hydrothermal-vent chemiosmosis (Lane, Martin, Russell), banded-iron formations and the Great Oxidation Event (2.4 Gya) — the deep-time record that supplies the weapon. Analytics that come up empty: metagenomic sequencing returning nothing, isotope-ratio mass spec, Raman, electron microscopy — nail why standard assays fail. Spread math: endospore and extremophile resistance (Deinococcus radiodurans, tardigrade cryptobiosis), biofilm/EPS resistance to sterilants, exponential doubling and R0, incineration/autoclave temperature limits.

**8. Opening image candidate** — The nitrogen seal on the Apollo 17 drive tube had held for a hundred years, and Alma broke it at 09:14 with a quarter-turn of the torque wrench, logging the mass, the pressure, and the time before she noticed the frost inside the manifold.

**9. Ending directive** — End on an act of cataloguing: Alma entering, in the register, the destruction she chose — a condition report on an object that no longer exists, the entry itself the only surviving specimen. Content open.

**10. Forbidden**
- The bloom given will or malice, a monster that "wants." It is a metabolism, weather-like, indifferent (cf. Overburden's refreeze).
- The preserve-it-for-science faction written as a glory-hungry villain; the keep-side must be sympathetic and partly right.
- The identification scene played as a full-explanation data dump; mechanism stays inferential, decoded but never wholly.
- Body-horror spectacle as the payload; the dread is a nominal checklist with one line wrong, not gore.
- Making Theia a lost civilization with artifacts and intent (Lagerstätte's and Second Eden's country). No ruins. Pre-DNA slime, not a people.

**11. Levers** — (a) Dual POV adding Kane, or a braided Hadean thread giving the redox war its own timeline — powerful, but the braid risks drifting toward Lagerstätte; hold as option. (b) Darker: containment fails; the book ends with the counter-ecology as a permanent quarantined occupation — a walled district of Washington where Earth now shares its chemistry, a hold-the-line, not-a-win close. (c) Scope compression: a 72-hour bottle thriller sealed in the museum basement, the city never breached — trade the epic response for unity of place.
