# Nº 40 — Understory · FAR (Tier C)

0. **The repair** — As skeletoned, the premise runs Nº39 Lagerstätte's engine — a deep-time sentinel keyed to a human trigger, culminating in the watchers' arrival ("contact with the stewards") — a monster-reveal the Tier A discipline forbids (31 ends at the threshold). The single change that promotes the book: **seal the vault.** The Interred are never woken and never shown; the novum's payload is the geochemical proof and the climatic wake-number, not a face. This severs Understory from Lagerstätte's arrival and Steerage's custody-of-species, leaving the one FAR book whose antagonist is a stratigraphic column and whose crisis is wholly among the living.

1. **Secretly about** — Inheriting a ruined house with the previous owners still asleep in the cellar: whether a precedent for your own catastrophe is a warning you can use or an alibi for giving up.

2. **Sibling rule** — Nº39 *Lagerstätte* (same deep-time sentinel, same human trigger). Distinction to enforce: Understory's watchers are **native, terrestrial, and forever unseen** — no arrival, no braided ancient-era POV; the deep past is reached only through cores and logs.

3. **The two questions** — *Outward:* how do you drill nine kilometres into 300 °C craton on a budget and a reputation that won't survive the claim, before the string, the crew, or the funding fails? *Inward:* whether Aluko can stop reading the deep past for an escape route and accept stewardship of a surface nobody signed over to him.

4. **Dyad** — *Equation:* the wake condition is a carbon threshold — atmospheric CO₂ and ocean-carbonate saturation climbing toward the same PETM-scale number the Interred fled; the drill clock races the ppm clock, and we are what drives the ppm. *Mirror:* Aluko and his partner Selma, who has decided to leave the drying land — a marriage split on stay-and-repair versus check-out, the Interred's choice in miniature.

5. **Shape** — Single POV, close third, Tayo Aluko, past tense. Structural device: each chapter headed by the day's survey telemetry — the present atmospheric/ocean-chemistry reading, in the program's logging format, climbing toward the threshold (the ledger counts up). Three parts: THE HORIZON / THE STRUCTURES / THE THRESHOLD. ~115,000 words, ~33 chapters.

6. **The novum's price** —
   - **Time / relationship:** the superdeep borehole is a multi-season, string-losing money pit; the field years spend Aluko's last window with Selma, who leaves before the vault is reached.
   - **Flesh:** a superheated brine-and-gas kick on the rig floor kills or maims a crew member — the vault's geothermal power source is also what can cook the hole.
   - **Money / standing:** the Silurian claim is career suicide; retraction pressure, defunded grants, his name a punchline — and the wake-number, once known, is politically radioactive enough to be buried, costing him a colleague or the truth.

7. **Science memo pointers** — The Silurian hypothesis (Schmidt & Frank 2018): δ¹³C excursions, plastic-derived and persistent-organic carbon, rare-earth and platinum-group depletion, reactive-nitrogen anomalies; chemostratigraphy; U-Pb and Ar-Ar dating. The real analog for the before-catastrophe and the wake-number is the **PETM** (~56 Ma, ~5–8 °C on a carbon pulse) — the book's science spine. Deep drilling: Kola Superdeep (stopped by heat and rock plasticity ~180 °C), ICDP practice, mud systems, elastomer/electronics temperature limits, stuck pipe, blowout preventers; geotherm ~25–30 °C/km. Deep-time isolation precedent (WIPP, Onkalo). Void detection at depth: seismic reflection, gravimetry, magnetotellurics, muon tomography. Siting must survive 59 Myr of subduction/erosion — stable Archean craton (Congo/Kaapvaal, Namibia). Stasis and 59-Myr information survival collide with cumulative radiogenic dose — spend the one implausibility here, honestly.

8. **Opening image candidate** — The core came up the pipe before dawn and Aluko split it on the logging table himself, running the wire saw down the long axis until the halves fell open on a centimetre of black he had no name for, and he logged its depth before he logged anything he felt about it.

9. **Ending directive** — End on Aluko making the choice physical on the borehole — a cap, a plug, a valve, a single logged value — the vault left as he chose to leave it; no steward seen, no wake witnessed. An act of stewardship or refusal, never a revelation.

10. **Forbidden** — (a) waking or showing the Interred; a steward emerging to deliver theme in speech. (b) A braided from-the-inside deep-past timeline (that is Lagerstätte). (c) Ancient-aliens / Atlantis mysticism — the before-people were a failed industrial species like us, not enlightened sages. (d) The climate allegory said aloud ("we *are* the before-people") — keep it in the geochemistry. (e) Structuring the "there was a before" as a withheld twist; the premise gives it away in Act I.

11. **Levers** — (a) dual POV: promote the pro-wake faction lead (or Selma) to counter-protagonist, arguing the Interred are our only hope. (b) darker: the vault is dead — stasis rotted 20 Myr ago, the instruments run on a dead hand, and the wake-condition is being met with no one home. (c) scope: compress to one field season and one borehole, a rig chamber-piece, or expand across the decades of the paradigm war.
