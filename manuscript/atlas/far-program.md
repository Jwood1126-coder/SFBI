# PROJECT FAR — The Program (Volume I)
### The shelf a future editor browses. Companion to ATLAS.md §G, which governs. Volume I of the premise dossier: fifty novels. v1, 2026-07-08.

## What FAR is

FAR is a shelf of fifty novels, not a story collection. Where THE GRAIN's projects draft to 5,000 words, every premise here is a novel-scale skeleton — a 100,000-to-130,000-word book with a three-act spine, a crew who practice a real trade, and one implausibility spent carefully. The **far voice** (ATLAS §G) is bible §2 with the reality law turned *up*: orbital mechanics, delta-v, comms lag, and closed-loop life support must survive an engineer, because the premise spends its whole novum budget on the cosmic thing and affords no second lie. Wonder is permitted at full register but only when earned by procedure — awe through an instrument read correctly, never an adjective — and dread is procedural, its scariest sentence a checklist with one line wrong. No book coins more than three terms.

Development follows the **Novel Protocol** — treatment, then arcs, then revision by act — defined in ATLAS §G and not restated here. This document sits above it, deciding *which* books, in *what* order, and which must never touch.

## Development sequence

**Develop these three first: 02 The Custodian, 01 Ballast, 14 The Last Lighthouse** — chosen against each other. 02 is the cheapest, cleanest proof of the thesis — one man, one habitat, closed-loop life support, competence with grief at its core — spending no novum budget, so a first team learns the voice before it gambles. 01 sits beside it in register but not in shape: crew labor and a dual-POV sacrifice run as a rostering document, a second intimate mode. 14 is the boldest claim, and the third book must make it: cosmic horror carried entirely by inference, the monster a graph, no ship ever launched. The slate thus shares one production discipline — no embodied cosmic other, minimal world-build — while spanning intimate grief, crew labor, and the epistemic sublime.

Held in A but sequenced *after* the slate: 16 Bait and 11 Bathypelagic carry stronger novums at higher risk, and their patterns recur across the most siblings — they read best once the voice is banked.

**Nearest promotion from Tier B: 33 Shade, 13 Seismograph, 47 The Ark of Zero.** Each promotes the same way — commission the treatment and confirm the *inward* question survives the novum at length. 33 is closest: its brief already specifies dual POV, coupled-telemetry chapter heads, four mission-phase parts. 13 owns the territory Tier A 31 vacates — a *running* machine that summons the knowing *in* and must cross the threshold, where 31 stops at the door. 47 carries the stewardship shelf's strongest engine: building, in secret, the vessel whose own arithmetic guarantees it will never carry you.

**Tiering, in five moves.** (1) Confirm 01, 02, 14 as the lead slate. (2) Treat 33 Shade as development-ready. (3) Elevate 13 Seismograph toward A as 31's complement, not its rival — no A book is displaced. (4) Hold 16 and 11 in A but sequence them fourth and fifth. (5) Flag 08 Analog as the A book that must not be a team's first. No Tier B brief revealed a book stronger than its A sibling; the closest call, 13 against 31, resolves to *both*, their split (transact vs. restrain at the threshold) making them additive.

## Collision watchlist

Siblings share a premise and split on one enforced axis; two that read alike must never develop back-to-back. The knots:

- **Instrument-horror hub (14):** 14↔13, 14↔15, 14↔18, 14↔20. Only 14 stays a pure graph.
- **Decipherment hub (31):** 31↔13, 31↔35, 31↔39, 31↔19, 31↔49. Only 31 ends at an uncrossed threshold.

## The cadence

Per the Novel Protocol: a full treatment cycle first — the 25-to-35 chapter map, character sheets, science memo, continuity ledger, both questions in a line each. Then drafting in arcs of three to four chapters, each agent reading treatment plus ledger plus the prior arc, the ledger updated every arc; a thirty-chapter book runs eight to ten arcs. Then revision by act, one lever per pass, Lever Zero always available. Before book N+1 opens, book N must be *closed* — ledger reconciled, science memo audited against an engineer, both questions answered — and its siblings must not be next in the queue. A fresh sibling's solutions get borrowed.

## Done criteria

A FAR novel is finished when it passes the project's audits. The **reality-law audit**: every orbital, thermal, radiation, comms-lag, and life-support number survives an engineer. The **voice audit**: bible §2 banned phrases at zero, no filtering, no epiphany endings, the three-term coinage budget respected, wonder earned only by procedure. The **two-question audit**: both the outward question (how do these survive this universe?) and the inward (what must they become to do it?) are answered, neither orphaned. The **ending audit**: the book closes on the brief's directed object, act, or line, never a realization. The **forbidden audit**: none of the brief's named ruin-moves committed — the cosmic other stays unembodied wherever the brief forbids it. The **sibling audit**: the book cannot be mistaken for its named twin. The **names audit**: every POV given name unique corpus-wide, per NAMES.md.
