# WHAT THE BODY KEEPS — Book architecture
### The page a future editor opens first. Companion to BIBLE.md §1; inherits §2 (voice law) and §0. Amend with dates.

## What this book is

WHAT THE BODY KEEPS is twelve stories in the 2029–2044 near future of THE GRAIN, each built on one instrument that makes a human interior legible — a mind furnished, two minds merged, a memory edited, a will decoded. It runs hotter and closer than any other book in the project: more skin, more kitchens.

Where THE GRAIN argued that instruments read *true* and the truth is never the thing, this book asks the price. Its instruments do not measure the interior from outside; they open it — and the argument is that legibility is not knowing, that a self read all the way through still keeps what no reading reaches, and the keeper is the body, not the record. It must not be THE GRAIN again: that was last things, elegiac, love as maintenance. This is the marriage bed and the delivery room, love as wanting — where ACREAGE stays civic and THE UNANSWERABLE cosmic, this one stays domestic and carnal.

## The arc

**Interior** opens. A woman who has never once pictured anything is given an inner eye — a *gaining*, so the book does not open in mourning as THE GRAIN nearly did. It poses the question: when the inside can finally be seen, is what shows the truth of a person, or newly furnished rooms?

**Duration** closes. A baker whose wife lived dilated years he could never enter loses her, and the next morning misjudges a proof — time got away from him, in the hands, not the record. It answers the opener: seen or unseen, the body keeps what the reading cannot hold; grief arrives as a chore of dough.

## Movements

Three parts of four; subjects mixed *within* each so none becomes a single-register trough (THE GRAIN's Part III error).

- **I. THE INTERIOR MADE LEGIBLE** — legibility proves an opening, not an answer. *Interior · The Hour · Next of Kin · Both.*
- **II. THE COST OF KNOWING** — once read, the interior sends a bill. *Proband · [OVERSHARE] · Mother Tongue · Left.*
- **III. THE KEEPING** — past every reading and edit, the body keeps its own record. *Custody · Sotto Voce · Amnion · Duration.*

**Splits from same-register neighbors.** The four brain-instrument pieces (Interior, Both, Left, Sotto Voce) hold positions 1, 4, 8, 10, never adjacent; the two dead-kin reconstructions (Mother Tongue, Duration) sit seven apart. **Amnion** (birth) sits at 11 to break the somber final run and rhyme with **Duration**: arrival then loss.

## Range requirements

Comedy: **Next of Kin** (docket-form paperwork against a biopsy). Formal daring: **Both** (stenotype transcript against a lettered board) and **Sotto Voce** (continuous single-take). Warmth/eros: **The Hour**, **Amnion**, the opener's gaining.

Two gaps trace to THE GRAIN's hardest lesson. First, **all eleven briefs are 3rd limited** (THE GRAIN required first person, documentary, dialogue, second person) — diversify by pulling **Both** to first person (lever b) or **Next of Kin** to document-built (lever c). Second, **the roster lacks a true major-key pole** — a young protagonist who *wants* and chases, gaining not losing. So, as THE GRAIN commissioned Nameplate:

> **Commission — OVERSHARE** (provisional). First person, present tense, ~4,200 words, two people in their twenties; a consumer descendant of the book's instruments — the cheap, faddish Hour merge, or a dating-market compatibility read — accelerates a courtship. Register: comic, erotic, propulsive, with momentum. **Forbidden:** death, obsoleted-trade elegy, grief notebook, heirloom worn smooth. The vitality pole; draft it before the elegiac stories pull the book monotone.

## Voice delta

BIBLE §2 holds entire. Amendments:

1. **Eros on the page.** Where THE GRAIN priced love as upkeep, this book may price it as wanting — the body rendered directly, never purple, never maintenance-metaphor by default.
2. **The domestic set is the worksite.** Rule 10 still binds, but the work is often the bed or the kitchen; a story may open with hands on a body, not a trade task.
3. **The function test bites harder** (rule 18). Each novum makes a *mind* legible and the plot turns on it, so it is glossed plainly and once; the interior stays mysterious.
4. **The major key is mandatory.** Two stories per assembly run major-key; no three same-register neighbors; POV varies.
5. **A new master image.** THE GRAIN over-used the notebook (nine times) and the worn talisman (sixteen); both rationed, the talisman-default banned. Here the master image is the body keeping what the record can't — the graft union, the caught weight, the left hand spelling its name.

**Calibration — a spouse touching a sleeping partner.**

*GRAIN voice (~70 w):* He checked the thermostat, sixty-eight, and came to bed the way he came to bed every night of the year. Nadia had left the lamp on for him and the covers turned back on his side. He set his glasses on the sill, squared to the edge, lay down in the two inches she always left him, and did not reach across them.

*BODY voice (~70 w):* She's asleep on her stomach, the sheet down at her waist, and I put my mouth between her shoulder blades where the day still smells like flour. She doesn't wake. I read the long muscle down her spine with one thumb, the knot under the left blade that means she stood too long, and I stay there, wanting nothing back, learning her in the dark by hand.

## Canon posture

**This book lives in the 2029–2044 GRAIN world** — same law, same weather, same civilizational retreat as background. Two rules from THE GRAIN's cold read are load-bearing: **year-stamp every story** so the arc reads 2032→2044, and let the instruments visibly *harden* across that span (clinic → statute → consumer fad), as the memo wished the index had.

**May echo, glancing only:** Pareon (The Hour), the consciousness index (Sotto Voce), Vestry AIs (Mother Tongue), the bright sky (Interior), timebase drift (Left), Grid Rationalization, the Adeyemi Signature. **Off-limits:** no GRAIN character crosses (bible rule); nothing load-bearing in THE GRAIN is load-bearing again — Pareon's home is Adherence, so it only glances; the Completed Life Act is barred, so The Hour never becomes an assisted-death rerun. **New BODY canon** (load-bearing once each, dated in-span): the consciousness merge, the inner-feed decoder, memory reconsolidation-editing, the decant pavilion, the Kinship Act — logged in a canon ledger the day a draft first names one.

## Commission order

Hardest and the arc's poles first; realist domestic pieces last.

1. **The Hour** — sets the merge canon and the sharpest function-test problem: a plot turning on an interior the reader may not see.
2. **Interior** (opener) and 3. **Duration** (closer) — fix the poles.
4. **OVERSHARE** (commission) — prove the major key before elegy accretes.
5. **Sotto Voce** — the riskiest register (a locked-in will; the assisted-death question THE GRAIN never paid off).
6. **Next of Kin** — the second release valve.
7–12. **Both, Mother Tongue, Amnion, Custody, Left, Proband** — the calibrated middle.

**Second look first.** *The Hour* — confirm a merge, not a Completed-Life rerun. *Sotto Voce* — decide whether the "tired sentence" raises assisted death, and pay off whatever it raises. *Both* — guard the forbidden alien-hand horror. *Proband* — closest to THE GRAIN of the eleven; push it hotter or it reads as GRAIN again.

## Done criteria

Inheriting THE GRAIN's audits, finished means:

- **Banned-phrase scan** (§2 rule 8) returns zero.
- **Name and prop greps** clear — the memo's prop class (candy, seed-cracking, scanners, worn talismans) plus this book's rations: notebook capped, talisman-default banned.
- **Phrase-frequency n-gram scan** clears the ration list ("the way," "neither of them said," "its own kind of," "did not X," the appositive-gloss dash before it turns metronome).
- **The function test** passes on every novum: what the plot turns on is understood, not merely believed.
- **Range and arc:** POV varied; two major-key stories, OVERSHARE among the first seven; no same-register neighbors; all twelve year-stamped.
- **Dyad named per story in the changelog** — equation and mirror; if either won't name, it is not done.
- **Cold-reader pass:** a context-free editor does not learn the move and start bracing.
