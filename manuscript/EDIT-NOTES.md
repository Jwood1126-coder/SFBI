# THE GRAIN — Editorial pass, draft one, chapters 1–8
### Method: read as a first reader, ask the questions a reader would ask, fix what the questions expose. Chapters 9–16 get the same pass when they land.

## Global findings

- **Voice law: held.** Zero banned phrases across all eight drafts (mechanically audited). No filtering verbs surfaced in close reading. Endings land on objects and acts throughout.
- **Canon discipline: held.** Each load-bearing canon item appears only in its assigned chapter; glancing echoes stayed glancing.
- **Reality law: exceeded expectations.** Ch. 4's Ohio pharmacy practice (OARRS, PDC measures, reverse distribution), ch. 6's conjunction-assessment procedure (Pc thresholds, covariance behavior), and ch. 8's occultation timing (VTI, chords, O–C residuals) would survive professional readers. Ch. 8 is set in the real Protection, Kansas, Comanche County, with correct geography.
- **A cross-chapter texture note for revision, not yet acted on:** Lou (ch. 1) and Reyes (ch. 6) both crack sunflower seeds into a container. Convergent detail from independent writers. Harmless twice; watch chs. 9–16 — if it appears a third time, change one.

## Reader questions asked, and what they exposed

**Ch. 1 — Familiar.** *"Is 90 his oxygen or his pulse?"* — the oximeter number was ambiguous and, read as SpO₂, alarming. Changed to an unambiguous pulse reading (68) with "the pulse line read." *"Whose divorce sent Ana to Chicago?"* — could be read as the parents'. Now "her divorce." *"Does Renata work at a bakery or teach piano?"* — text supports both (bakery mornings, lessons at four); left alone; watch in revision.

**Ch. 2 — Because.** *"How does she just get her mother's hospice chart?"* — "I am licensed to read it" was glib; records don't work that way. Now Diane's kept copy, mailed "without one question, which was its own answer" — truer to law and to Diane.

**Ch. 3 — Fluent.** *"How did an earbud translate six syllables into three sentences?"* — the climax had the device rendering intent, which a translator cannot do; the novum's honesty is the story's spine. Fixed: David now breaks past his rehearsed phrase into English — his whole tongue — and the bud carries that, honestly. Sets up the ending harder: at the bath he *has* English available and chooses the bad Japanese instead. *"Mio came to that sound since she could walk — but grandma arrived when Mio was five?"* — timeline slip; now "since the winter the woman arrived."

**Ch. 4 — Adherence.** *"How did Delia's abandoned bottle get into his drawer at the store?"* — one-sentence origin added (she set it out with the expired sunscreens for tossing; he carried it to the store instead). Note: the draft replaced the premise's insurance audit with Owen pulling her profile himself — an improvement; he chooses to look. Recorded here as an accepted deviation.

**Ch. 5 — Sibship.** *"She set three rounds at load-in but the chart has four rounds?"* — continuity; now four. RSVP math checks (19 attending + 4 refusals + 8 opened-unanswered + 9 unopened = 40 others). The ending lands the brief's directive exactly: Table four.

**Ch. 6 — Conjunction.** **Continuity conflict with ch. 7:** Ade's station was drafted as "Meridian" — the same name as ch. 7's station, which deorbits whole with ceremony; ch. 6's suffers a fatal debris strike. Renamed to **Vireo** per the original brief (two instances). Note: the draft pulled lever (a) — the unfinished chess game — at draft time; it works; recorded as pulled.

**Ch. 7 — Deorbit.** *"Why does the whole world have ears on this loop? What almost happened here?"* — the draft internalized the premise's public refusal so completely it never occurred on the page, leaving Onyx's message and "talk to me, not to them" hanging from nothing. The draft's quietness is superior to a standoff and was kept — but one beat was inserted (GMT 130/04:52): the refusal stated once, flatly, for the log, never repeated. Everything downstream now reads as consequence. The gray cylinder (31 g, no barcode) and its final placement in the cupola, facing Earth, bound for Point Nemo with the station: not named anywhere, correctly. Do not name it in any revision.

**Ch. 8 — The Ellipse.** *"Why don't they just push the rock?"* — the writer's own second pass answered this in scene (the gym meeting; a push walks the impact point back through everyone the corridor already spared). One orphaned callback ("the way Dell had counted March twice") referenced ch. 4's imagery with no referent in this story — cut. Word count rose to ~4,100 of a commissioned 5,500; the story is whole at this length; do not pad it.
